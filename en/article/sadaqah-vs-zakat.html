Sadaqah vs Zakat: What's the Difference?

What Is Zakat?

Zakat is an obligatory annual payment of 2.5% of qualifying wealth. It is one of the Five Pillars of Islam and must be given to specific categories of people.

To be liable, a Muslim must own wealth above the nisab threshold for a full lunar year. Zakat purifies wealth and reminds the giver that everything belongs to Allah.

What Is Sadaqah?

Sadaqah is voluntary charity. It can be any amount, given at any time, to almost anyone in need. It includes money, food, clothing, kind words, and even removing harm from the road.

The Prophet (peace be upon him) said that smiling at your brother is sadaqah. This shows that sadaqah is broad and reflects a generous character.

Key Differences

Zakat is obligatory, calculated at a fixed rate, and paid to specific recipients. Sadaqah is voluntary, unlimited in amount, and can be given to anyone.

Zakat requires the passage of one lunar year over the wealth. Sadaqah has no timing requirement. Zakat is due on savings and trade goods; sadaqah can come from any lawful income.

Who Receives Zakat and Sadaqah?

The Qur'an lists eight categories of Zakat recipients, including the poor, the needy, those employed to collect it, new Muslims, those in debt, travelers, and those in the cause of Allah.

Sadaqah has no fixed categories. It can support orphanages, mosques, education, disaster relief, neighbours, or public welfare projects.

Impact on the Giver and Society

Both Zakat and Sadaqah reduce inequality, build community trust, and purify the soul from greed. Zakat provides a structured safety net, while Sadaqah allows spontaneous compassion.

Paying Zakat correctly requires calculation and knowledge. You can read our Zakat calculation guide for help.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Sadaqah the same as Zakat?

No. Zakat is obligatory with fixed rules; Sadaqah is voluntary and flexible.

Can Sadaqah be given to family members?

Yes, as long as they are not already financially responsible for you, such as parents or children.

Can Zakat be given to mosques?

No, mosques are not among the eight categories of Zakat recipients, though they may receive Sadaqah.

Do I need to wait a year to give Sadaqah?

No. Sadaqah can be given at any time and in any amount.

What if I cannot afford Zakat?

If your wealth is below the nisab, you are not required to pay Zakat, but you can still give Sadaqah.
