Saudi Family Visit Visa: Application, Extension, Rules

doyouknow.app Editorial Team2026-07-1014 min read

Editorial note: This guide was checked against official Saudi government sources and service portals on 2026-07-10. Fees, procedures, and eligibility rules can change, so confirm details through the official channels linked below before acting.

What Is the Saudi Family Visit Visa?

The Saudi family visit visa allows expatriates living in the Kingdom to invite close family members—such as parents, spouses, and children—for a temporary stay. It is one of the most commonly used visit visa categories and is issued by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs through its online visa platform. The visa is usually valid for 90 days from the date of entry, with the possibility of extension under certain conditions.

Unlike a work or residence visa, the family visit visa does not grant the right to work or obtain an iqama. Visitors must leave the country before the visa expires or apply for an extension if eligible. Overstaying can result in fines, deportation, and a ban on future entry. Understanding the rules before applying helps avoid problems at airports and during the visit.

The sponsor must meet eligibility criteria, including a valid iqama, a suitable profession, and sufficient income. The exact rules and required documents can change, so applicants should always check the latest instructions on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs website or through the sponsor's Absher account.

Who Can Apply and Who Can Be Invited

Family visit visas are generally issued to immediate family members of resident expatriates. This typically includes parents, spouses, children, and sometimes parents-in-law. Brothers and sisters may also be eligible under certain conditions, but the rules are stricter and may require additional documentation or higher income.

The sponsor must hold a valid iqama and usually needs to have a profession and salary that meet the current requirements. Some professions are excluded from sponsoring family visit visas. The sponsor must also have a clean legal record and no outstanding fines or violations that could block the application.

Visitors must have valid passports, usually with at least six months of validity remaining. Some nationalities may need additional documents, such as medical insurance or vaccination certificates. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs platform will list the exact requirements based on the visitor's nationality and relationship to the sponsor.

How to Apply Step by Step

The application begins on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs visa services portal. The sponsor creates an application, enters the visitor's personal details, selects the relationship, and uploads the required documents. These documents usually include a copy of the sponsor's iqama, the visitor's passport, and proof of relationship such as a marriage or birth certificate.

After submitting the application, the system generates a visa authorization number. The visitor then uses this number to complete the visa issuance process at a Saudi embassy or consulate abroad, or through an authorized visa center. In some cases, the visa can be issued electronically without visiting an embassy. The visitor should print the visa and carry it when traveling.

Travel insurance is often required and may be purchased during the application process. The visitor should also check health requirements, such as vaccinations, before departure. Airlines may ask to see the visa, return ticket, and proof of accommodation before boarding. Keeping digital and printed copies of all documents is strongly recommended.

What Happens After Arrival

Upon arrival in Saudi Arabia, the visitor goes through immigration control. The officer will check the visa, passport, and sometimes ask about the purpose and duration of the visit. The visitor may be fingerprinted and photographed. Once admitted, the visitor can stay for the period allowed by the visa.

The sponsor should help the visitor with accommodation and registration if required. Visitors staying in hotels may need to provide passport details at check-in. If staying in a private home, the sponsor should keep a copy of the visitor's passport and visa in case authorities request them. Visitors should also be aware of local laws and customs during their stay.

It is important to track the visa expiry date. The allowed stay is counted from the date of entry, not the date of visa issuance. Overstaying even by one day can result in fines. Visitors who need more time should begin the extension process well before the expiry date.

How to Extend a Family Visit Visa

Family visit visas can sometimes be extended through the sponsor's Absher account. The extension option depends on the visa type, the visitor's nationality, and current rules. Some visas allow a single extension of 90 days, while others may allow multiple shorter extensions. The extension fee must be paid before the new validity is granted.

To extend, the sponsor logs into Absher, selects the visa extension service, chooses the visitor, and pays the fee. The system will confirm whether the extension is approved. If approved, the visitor can remain in Saudi Arabia for the additional period. If the extension is not possible, the visitor must leave the country before the original visa expires.

Not all visit visas are extendable. Attempting to extend an unsupported visa type will result in rejection. In cases where the visitor cannot leave on time due to an emergency, the sponsor should contact the Jawazat office or the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for guidance. Overstaying while waiting for a decision is risky and should be avoided.

Fees, Insurance, and Costs

The cost of a family visit visa includes the visa fee, insurance premium, and any service charges from visa centers. Fees vary by nationality and visa duration. Insurance is mandatory for most visitors and covers emergency medical treatment during the stay. The insurance must be purchased from an approved provider.

Extension fees are additional and are usually paid per extension request. If the visa expires and the visitor overstays, fines are charged for each day of overstay. These fines must be paid before the visitor can leave the country or apply for a new visa. Airlines may deny boarding if the visa is expired or overstayed.

Sponsors should budget for all costs before applying. Some employers reimburse visa fees for immediate family members, but this is not guaranteed. Checking the latest fee schedule on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs website helps avoid surprises.

Important Rules and Restrictions

Family visit visa holders cannot work in Saudi Arabia. Taking paid employment on a visit visa is illegal and can result in fines, detention, and deportation for both the visitor and the sponsor. Visitors should also avoid activities that require a residence permit, such as opening a bank account or signing long-term rental contracts.

The visa is for family visits only. Visitors should not use it for business activities or religious tourism unless the visa explicitly allows those purposes. Some visit visas are tied to a specific entry type—single or multiple entry—and sponsors should confirm this before travel. A single-entry visa becomes invalid if the visitor leaves Saudi Arabia.

Visitors must respect Saudi laws and social norms. Dress codes, public behavior rules, and photography restrictions apply to everyone. Visitors should also register with the sponsor's local authorities if required. Following these rules ensures a safe and trouble-free visit.

Common Problems and Solutions

A common problem is a rejected application due to missing or incorrect documents. Sponsors should double-check names, passport numbers, and relationship documents before submitting. If the application is rejected, the system usually provides a reason, and the sponsor can correct the error and reapply.

Another issue is difficulty extending the visa. If Absher does not show an extension option, the visa may not be extendable. In that case, the visitor must leave before expiry. Some sponsors try to issue a new visit visa immediately after departure, but this depends on current rules and may require a waiting period.

Lost passports during the visit should be reported to the police and the visitor's embassy immediately. The sponsor should also inform Jawazat. Emergency travel documents may be issued by the embassy, but the exit process can take time. Keeping copies of all documents in a secure cloud storage helps in such situations.

Visit Visa Types and Entry Rules

The Saudi family visit visa is not the only type of visit visa. The government also offers commercial visit visas, tourist e-visas, and personal visit visas. Each type has its own purpose and restrictions. A family visit visa is intended specifically for visiting close relatives who are legally resident in Saudi Arabia.

Entry rules can vary by nationality. Some visitors receive a single-entry visa, while others receive multiple-entry visas that allow several trips within the validity period. The number of entries is usually decided by the authorities during processing. Check the visa sticker or electronic document carefully to understand what is allowed.

Visitors must enter Saudi Arabia before the visa’s “enter before” date, even if the allowed stay is longer. Missing this date means the visa becomes invalid and a new application is needed. Planning travel dates before applying helps avoid wasted fees.

Document Attestation and Translation

Documents that prove family relationships may need attestation before they are accepted. Birth certificates, marriage certificates, and family books sometimes require attestation by the issuing country’s foreign ministry, the Saudi embassy, and sometimes the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Saudi Arabia after arrival.

If the document is not in Arabic or English, a certified translation may be required. Some embassies provide translation services, while others require a local certified translator. Translations should be accurate and complete, because errors can delay or reject the visa application.

Keep both the original and attested copies of important documents. The Ministry of Foreign Affairs visa platform may ask for scanned copies during the online application. Having clear, high-quality scans ready speeds up the process.

Health Insurance and Medical Requirements

Most family visit visa applicants must purchase health insurance from an approved Saudi provider. The insurance covers emergency medical treatment during the visit. It does not usually cover routine check-ups or pre-existing conditions unless specifically included. Read the policy details before buying.

The insurance is linked to the visa application. If the applicant does not purchase insurance, the visa may not be issued. Some platforms allow the sponsor to buy the insurance on behalf of the visitor. The insurance certificate should be printed and carried during travel.

Visitors should also check vaccination requirements, especially for diseases such as meningitis during Hajj and Umrah seasons or COVID-19 if rules are active. Carry vaccination certificates if required. This protects the visitor and makes immigration clearance smoother.

Airport Arrival and Immigration Questions

When arriving at a Saudi airport, visitors should have their passport, visa printout, return ticket, and accommodation details ready. Immigration officers may ask about the relationship to the sponsor, the length of stay, and the purpose of the visit. Answering clearly and politely helps the process go smoothly.

Officers may take fingerprints and a photograph. Visitors with difficulty providing fingerprints due to age or medical reasons should inform the officer. Minors usually travel with a parent or legal guardian who handles immigration procedures.

After clearing immigration, visitors collect luggage and proceed through customs. Certain items are restricted or prohibited, including alcohol, pork products, and large amounts of cash. Declaring required items avoids fines and confiscation.

Converting a Visit Visa to Residence

In some cases, a family visit visa can be converted to a residence visa, allowing the visitor to obtain an iqama. This usually applies to spouses and children of residents who meet sponsorship requirements. The process involves changing the visa status through the Ministry of Interior and paying the required fees.

The sponsor must meet salary and profession requirements. The visitor must pass a medical test and have valid insurance. The conversion must be completed before the visit visa expires. Because rules change, consult Absher or Jawazat for the latest eligibility criteria.

Not all visit visas can be converted. Trying to convert a tourist or commercial visit visa to residence may result in rejection and penalties. Always confirm the correct visa type for your long-term plans before applying.

Hosting Etiquette and Local Customs

Hosting family members in Saudi Arabia comes with social responsibilities. Visitors should understand local dress codes, prayer times, and public behavior rules. While visitors are not expected to know every custom, showing respect for local culture makes the visit more pleasant.

During Ramadan, eating, drinking, or smoking in public during daylight hours is not allowed. Visitors should plan meals privately during fasting hours. Restaurants may be closed or have limited hours during the day. Understanding these rhythms helps avoid awkward situations.

Family visits are also a good time to explore Saudi heritage sites, malls, and restaurants. However, visitors should always carry their passport and visa copies. Some attractions require tickets booked in advance. The Visit Saudi website is a useful resource for planning activities.

Travel Insurance Beyond Health

In addition to mandatory health insurance, visitors may want travel insurance that covers trip cancellation, lost luggage, and flight delays. Standard health insurance does not pay for these problems. Travel insurance can be purchased from international providers before departure.

Review the policy exclusions carefully. Some policies do not cover adventure activities or pre-existing medical conditions. Others have limits on electronics and valuables. Choose a plan that matches your travel style and the value of items you are bringing.

Keep a copy of the insurance policy and emergency contact numbers on your phone. If you need to make a claim, document the incident with photos, receipts, and police reports when required. Quick reporting helps claims get processed faster.

Options If a Visitor Needs to Stay Longer

Sometimes family emergencies or travel disruptions require a visitor to stay beyond the original visa. The first step is to check whether an extension is possible through Absher. If extension is not available, the visitor may need to leave and reapply for a new visa after a waiting period.

In exceptional cases, such as medical emergencies, the sponsor can contact Jawazat or the Ministry of Foreign Affairs to explain the situation. Supporting documents from a hospital or relevant authority may help. Approval is not guaranteed, and the visitor should avoid overstaying while waiting for a decision.

Track the visa expiry date carefully and plan travel in advance. Last-minute extensions are risky and may not be approved. Paying overstay fines does not remove the negative record, so compliance is always the best policy.

Sponsoring In-Laws and Extended Family

Sponsoring parents-in-law, siblings, or other relatives is possible under stricter rules. The sponsor must usually show a higher income and may need additional documents proving the relationship. Some categories of relatives are only eligible for short visits and cannot be converted to residence.

The Ministry of Foreign Affairs platform will indicate which relationships are allowed for the sponsor’s nationality and profession. Rules can change, so do not rely on outdated advice. If the platform rejects the relationship, the family member may need to apply for a different visa type.

Extended family visits should be planned well in advance. Processing times may be longer, and additional attestation may be required. Starting early reduces stress and increases the chance of approval.

Understanding Family Visit Visa Rejections

Visa rejections can happen for many reasons: missing documents, unqualified sponsor profession, insufficient income, unclear relationship proof, or security checks. The online system often provides a rejection code that explains the cause. Read the reason carefully before reapplying.

If the rejection seems incorrect, the sponsor can contact the Ministry of Foreign Affairs call center or visit a visa center for clarification. Sometimes a small correction, such as a clearer scan or updated passport, is enough for approval.

Avoid reapplying repeatedly with the same incorrect information. Each application costs money, and multiple rejections may raise flags. Take time to fix the underlying issue before submitting again.

Frequently asked questions

Who can sponsor a family visit visa?

A resident with a valid iqama, eligible profession, and sufficient income can sponsor parents, spouse, children, and sometimes in-laws.

How long is the family visit visa valid?

Usually 90 days from entry, but this can vary by visa type and nationality.

Can a family visit visa be extended?

Some visas can be extended through Absher for an additional period, depending on current rules.

Can visitors work on a family visit visa?

No. Working on a visit visa is illegal and can lead to deportation and penalties.

What documents are required?

Typically the sponsor’s iqama, visitor’s passport, proof of relationship, and travel insurance.

What happens if the visa expires?

The visitor must leave or extend before expiry. Overstaying results in fines and possible entry bans.

Can the visa be issued online?

The application is submitted online through the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, but some visitors must visit an embassy or visa center.
