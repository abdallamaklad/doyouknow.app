Did You Know the UAE Has a Minister for Happiness?

Here is something that sounds made up but is not: in 2016, the UAE created a government position called Minister of State for Happiness. An actual cabinet role with a budget and a national mission to make people happier. The UAE does not just build skyscrapers — it builds ministries for good feelings.

Who Is the UAE Minister for Happiness?

The first uae minister for happiness was Ohood Al Roumi. She was appointed in February 2016 by Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum. She also held the portfolio for Minister of State for Youth Affairs, making her one of the most forward-thinking figures in the cabinet.

Al Roumi came from economic policy and strategic planning. She was not a therapist. She was a government executive who believed happiness is a measurable outcome of good governance.

What Does the Minister of Happiness Actually Do?

The minister of happiness uae role is not about handing out free ice cream. It is about embedding happiness into government policy. The mission covers three core areas:

  1. Policy Integration — Making sure every government department considers citizen happiness when designing services and laws.
  1. Measurement — Developing a national happiness index and tracking how people feel about healthcare, education, and public spaces.
  1. Culture Change — Training government employees to deliver services with positivity and empathy.

The UAE Happiness Agenda: More Than a Title

The uae happiness agenda launched the National Happiness and Positivity Programme, which includes:

  • The Happiness Meter — a real-time feedback system in government offices where citizens rate satisfaction after every interaction.
  • The National Wellbeing Strategy — a long-term plan to improve quality of life across health, education, and environment.
  • Happiness in Schools — curriculum changes that teach emotional intelligence to children.
  • Chief Happiness Officers — government entities appoint internal officers who monitor happiness for both employees and the public.

Did It Work?

The results are mixed, but the ambition is real. The UAE consistently ranks in the top 20–30 countries in the World Happiness Report, which is impressive for a young nation in a turbulent region. The infrastructure, safety, and economic opportunity help.

Critics argue that a government cannot legislate happiness. Supporters say that even symbolic commitment forces institutions to prioritize human experience over pure efficiency. Either way, the UAE is one of the few countries that even tries.

Why This Matters to Visitors

If you visit the UAE, you will notice the difference. Airport staff smile more. Government service centers feel welcoming. Public spaces like parks and the Dubai Mall fountain are engineered for joy, not just function. The uae happiness ministry philosophy trickles down into daily life.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the current Minister of Happiness in the UAE?

Ohood Al Roumi was the original Minister of State for Happiness. The portfolio has been integrated into broader wellbeing strategies, but the mission continues.

What is the UAE happiness agenda?

It is a national program that measures, tracks, and improves citizen happiness through policy changes and public service design.

Does the UAE rank high in global happiness?

Yes, the UAE consistently ranks in the top 20–30 countries in the World Happiness Report, thanks to strong safety and infrastructure.

Is the Minister of Happiness a real job?

Yes, it was a real cabinet-level position created in 2016. It was one of the first such roles in the world.

Why did the UAE create a happiness ministry?

The UAE leadership believes that happiness is a key indicator of national success and good governance should be measured by quality of life, not just GDP.
