Tawakkalna App: What It Does and Why Residents Need It

doyouknow.app Editorial Team2026-07-0917 min read

Editorial note: This guide was checked against official Saudi government sources and service portals on 2026-07-09. App features, procedures, and eligibility rules can change, so confirm details through the official channels linked below before acting.

What Is Tawakkalna and Why It Matters

Tawakkalna is Saudi Arabia's official national mobile application for digital identity, health status, and government services. Developed by the Saudi Data and Artificial Intelligence Authority (SDAIA) in cooperation with the Ministry of Interior and the Ministry of Health, the app acts as a single gateway that connects residents to many public and private services. For anyone living in Saudi Arabia on an iqama or a national ID card, Tawakkalna has become an everyday tool rather than an optional convenience. The name itself reflects trust and reliability, which is what the government intends the platform to represent as Saudi Arabia moves toward a fully digital society.

During the global health emergency, Tawakkalna became widely known for showing health status, vaccination records, and movement permits. Since then, its role has expanded. Today the app stores a digital version of your identity documents, lets you book government appointments, shows your insurance and vehicle information, and provides access to services ranging from school enrollment to event tickets. Businesses, government offices, airports, and hospitals often ask to see a Tawakkalna status or QR code before providing service. As more organizations go paperless, the QR code is becoming the preferred proof of identity and eligibility.

Understanding how Tawakkalna works saves time and prevents frustration. A resident who cannot log in, whose status does not update, or whose documents are not linked may be turned away at a clinic, office, or border crossing. This guide explains what the app does, how to register, how it connects to your iqama, health insurance, and Absher account, and how to solve the most common problems.

Who Needs Tawakkalna

Almost everyone living in Saudi Arabia will interact with Tawakkalna at some point. The app is designed for Saudi citizens and residents. If you hold a valid Saudi national ID or iqama, you should have an active account. Visitors on tourist or business visas may not need the app for short stays, but they may be asked to install it in some situations, such as attending large events or entering certain facilities. Employers and schools increasingly expect residents to show their Tawakkalna profile.

Each family member who has an ID or iqama should ideally have their own Tawakkalna account or be managed through a guardian's account when appropriate. Parents can add children to their account in some cases, while sponsored dependents should set up their own profiles using their own credentials. Companies may also ask employees to keep their Tawakkalna status updated, especially in sectors where health clearance or permit verification is required. Universities and training centers may similarly require students to show a valid status before attending classes or examinations.

The app is particularly important for residents who need to visit government buildings, attend medical appointments, travel domestically or internationally, or enter venues that require identity verification. Because many services now ask for a Tawakkalna QR code instead of a physical card, having the app ready on your phone is almost as important as carrying your iqama.

How to Register and Activate Your Account

Before you begin, make sure you are downloading the genuine application. The official Tawakkalna app is published by the relevant Saudi authorities and is available on the Apple App Store and Google Play Store. Avoid unofficial copies or third-party websites that claim to offer the app, because they may compromise your personal data. Registration begins with a mobile phone number. Download the Tawakkalna app from the official Apple App Store or Google Play Store, then open it and select your preferred language. The app supports Arabic and English, and you can switch languages later in the settings. Enter your mobile number when prompted; you will receive a one-time password by SMS to confirm ownership of the number.

Next, the app asks for your identity details. Saudi citizens enter their national ID number, while residents enter their iqama number. The system validates this information against the National Information Center. If your records are up to date and your mobile number is registered in your name or your sponsor's records, verification is usually instant. If the system cannot match your details, you may need to update your mobile number through Absher or visit a service center.

After identity verification, you create a password and set up any optional security features such as biometric login or a PIN. Some users are also asked to take a selfie for facial recognition, especially when accessing sensitive services. Once activation is complete, your home screen displays your name, ID or iqama number, and a scannable QR code. You should also receive a confirmation message. Keep your login credentials secure and avoid sharing your password, because the app grants access to personal health, travel, and identity data. Writing the password down or storing it in an unencrypted note on your phone increases the risk of unauthorized access.

Linking Your Iqama, Absher, and Health Records

Tawakkalna does not exist in isolation. It pulls data from Absher, the National Information Center, the Ministry of Health, and the Council of Health Insurance. This integration is what makes the app powerful, but it also means that errors or delays in other systems can appear inside Tawakkalna. The most important link is between your iqama and your mobile number, because Tawakkalna uses that pairing to identify you.

To check whether your records are linked correctly, open the profile or identity section of the app. You should see your full name, nationality, date of birth, ID or iqama number, and employer or sponsor name. If any detail is wrong, the correction usually needs to be made in Absher or through your sponsor, not inside Tawakkalna itself. For example, if your profession or sponsor name is outdated, your employer must update the record in the Ministry of Human Resources system.

Health records are linked automatically when you use government healthcare services or receive vaccines through approved providers. Your vaccination history, test results, and insurance status appear in the health section if the provider has reported the data to the Ministry of Health or the Council of Health Insurance. If you have private health insurance, make sure the policy is registered with the council, because unregistered policies may not show up in Tawakkalna. In some cases, a missing insurance record can block access to services that require proof of coverage.

Everyday Use Cases: Appointments, Permits, and Travel

One of the most common uses of Tawakkalna is booking and managing government appointments. Instead of visiting an office and waiting in line, residents can select the service they need, choose a date and time, and receive a digital ticket. Appointments for passport services, traffic offices, civil affairs, and other government departments can often be scheduled through the app or through linked services that accept Tawakkalna authentication.

The app also handles various types of permits. During emergency periods, movement permits were essential. Today, permits for events, gatherings, or specific work sites may still be issued or verified through Tawakkalna. Event organizers commonly scan attendees' QR codes at the entrance. If your status is not valid, you may be denied entry even if you hold a ticket.

For travel, Tawakkalna provides health and identity verification that airlines and border authorities can check. Before an international trip, open the app and confirm that your status is active, your passport details are current, and any required health documents are displayed. Some residents have missed flights because their Tawakkalna status expired or did not match their travel documents. Checking the app a day or two before departure reduces last-minute stress. Some travelers also take a screenshot of their active status as a backup, although the live QR code is what most authorities require.

Health Status, Vaccines, and Medical Services

The health section of Tawakkalna is one of its most frequently used features. It can display your vaccination record, including doses received in Saudi Arabia and some records imported from abroad. It may also show recent test results, recovery certificates, and your general immune status. Clinics and hospitals often scan the health QR code during registration or admission.

Having a visible vaccination or health status is still required in some settings, such as attending certain events, entering government buildings, or traveling to specific destinations. The rules change over time, so the safest approach is to keep your vaccinations up to date and check the app before you go anywhere that may ask for verification. If a dose is missing from the app, contact the healthcare provider that administered it and ask them to upload the record to the Ministry of Health system.

Beyond COVID-related records, Tawakkalna increasingly connects to broader healthcare services. Some users can view their insurance provider, policy number, and covered dependents. In the future, more features such as appointment reminders, prescription records, and health risk assessments may be added. For now, treat the health section as a quick reference and carry your physical insurance card when visiting a provider, because not every clinic accepts a digital Tawakkalna record alone.

Privacy, Data Security, and Permissions

Tawakkalna collects and displays sensitive personal information, including your ID number, health status, location history in some cases, and vaccination details. SDAIA has stated that the app follows national data-protection standards and encrypts information in transit and at rest. However, users also have a responsibility to protect their own accounts and understand what they are sharing. Review the app's privacy policy periodically, because it may change as new features are introduced. If you stop using the app, you cannot fully delete government records, but you can revoke optional permissions from your phone settings to limit data collection.

When you install the app, review the permissions it requests. Tawakkalna may ask for location access, camera access, Bluetooth, and notifications. Location and Bluetooth were heavily used during health emergency periods for contact tracing and zone-based alerts. In normal use, you can often limit these permissions if you are comfortable doing so, but some features such as venue check-in or travel verification may require them. Notifications are useful for appointment reminders and status updates.

Never share your login password or SMS codes with anyone, including callers claiming to represent the government or technical support. Official agencies will not ask for your password. If you need someone else to help you with the app, use the official dependent or guardian features rather than handing over your credentials. If you suspect your account has been compromised, change your password immediately and contact Tawakkalna support through the official channels listed in the app.

Troubleshooting Common Tawakkalna Problems

Login failures are one of the most common issues. If the app rejects your password, make sure you are using the correct language keyboard and that caps lock is off. Use the password-reset option if needed; the reset link is sent to your registered mobile number. If you no longer have access to that number, you must update it through Absher or at a service center before you can recover the account.

Another frequent problem is a status that does not update after a recent change. For example, your vaccination may not appear, or your permit may still show as expired even after renewal. In most cases, the delay is caused by the upstream system that feeds data into Tawakkalna, not by the app itself. Wait a few hours, then check again. If the issue persists for more than a day, contact the relevant authority: the Ministry of Health for health records, your sponsor or the Ministry of Human Resources for iqama updates, or the issuing office for permits.

App crashes, slow loading, or display errors can usually be fixed by clearing the app cache, updating to the latest version, or reinstalling the app. Make sure your phone's operating system is up to date, because older versions may not support the latest security features. If you receive a message saying your device is not supported, check the official Tawakkalna website for the minimum system requirements. Installing the app on a recently updated phone avoids most compatibility problems.

Tips for Getting the Most from Tawakkalna

Think of Tawakkalna as a digital wallet for your government identity. Keep the app updated. New versions often include security patches, new services, and bug fixes. Enable automatic updates if possible. Set up a strong password and consider using biometric login for faster access. Add the app's support number or official social media channels to your contacts so you can reach help quickly if something goes wrong.

Check your profile regularly for outdated information. If you renew your iqama, change your mobile number, or update your insurance provider, confirm that these changes appear in Tawakkalna within a reasonable time. Catching discrepancies early prevents problems when you need the app at a clinic, airport, or government office.

Finally, take time to learn which services are available inside the app and which still require a visit to Absher or a physical office. Tawakkalna is convenient, but it does not replace every government process. For complex issues such as sponsorship transfer, legal disputes, or major corrections to your residency record, you will still need to work through the appropriate ministry or service center.

Frequently asked questions

What is Tawakkalna used for?

Tawakkalna is Saudi Arabia's official app for digital identity, health status, government appointments, permits, travel verification, and access to public and private services.

Do tourists need Tawakkalna?

Short-term visitors usually do not need the app, but they may be asked to install it for specific events, venues, or facilities that require identity or health verification.

How do I register for Tawakkalna?

Download the official app, verify your mobile number with an SMS code, enter your national ID or iqama number, and complete the identity verification process.

Why is my vaccination record missing in Tawakkalna?

The provider may not have uploaded the record to the Ministry of Health system. Contact the clinic or hospital that administered the dose and ask them to report it.

Can I use Tawakkalna without an Absher account?

You can install and use the app, but some features depend on records that are managed through Absher. If your mobile number or identity details are not matched, you may need to update them in Absher first.

What should I do if my Tawakkalna status is wrong?

Check whether the error comes from your iqama, health insurance, or health records. Update the source system or contact the relevant authority, because Tawakkalna usually reflects data from other government systems.

Is my data safe in Tawakkalna?

SDAIA states that the app uses encryption and national data-protection standards. Protect your account by using a strong password, not sharing login codes, and keeping the app updated.
