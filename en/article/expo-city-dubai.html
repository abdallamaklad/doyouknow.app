Expo City Dubai: What Happened After the Expo?

Six months of global attention ended in March 2022. But the real story started after the gates closed.

Expo City Dubai isn't a ghost town. It isn't a abandoned fairground. It's a completely reimagined city district — and it's already more interesting than most people realize. If you're wondering what happened after Expo 2020, the answer is: a lot. And most of it is open right now.

What Is Expo City Dubai?

Expo City Dubai is a 4.38 square kilometer master-planned city built on the site of Expo 2020 Dubai. It opened to the public in October 2022, just months after the Expo closed. The plan was always to create a permanent legacy — not just a memory.

The district combines residential, commercial, cultural, and entertainment spaces. It hosts events, conferences, and exhibitions year-round. The iconic Al Wasl Dome still stands. The pavilions have new lives. And the sustainability district is still generating clean energy.

What Can You Visit Right Now?

Al Wasl Plaza

The 130-meter-wide dome is still the heart of the city. It's the largest 360-degree projection surface in the world. During the Expo, it hosted shows watched by millions. Today, it's an events venue and a gathering space. Walking underneath it at night is still magical. The projection shows run on weekends.

The Pavilions That Stayed Open

Several country pavilions remain open as museums and cultural centers. The UAE Pavilion — designed to resemble a falcon in flight — now hosts national exhibitions. The Sustainability Pavilion, called Terra, is an interactive museum about climate and ecology. The Mobility Pavilion, Alif, explores the future of transport. The Opportunity Pavilion, Mission Possible, focuses on creating positive change.

These aren't empty buildings. They're fully operational museums with new exhibits, workshops, and educational programs. If you missed the Expo, you can still experience the best of it.

Surreal Water Feature

The Surreal water feature, designed by WET Design, is a mesmerizing fountain and light show. It was a crowd favorite during the Expo, and it's still running daily. It's free to watch and makes for incredible photos after sunset.

The Garden in the Sky

This 55-meter-tall rotating observation tower gives you panoramic views of the entire district. It was one of the most popular attractions during the Expo, and it's still operating. The ride takes about 10 minutes and offers a unique perspective on the master plan.

Jubilee Park and Children's Areas

The green spaces are open to the public. Families picnic on the lawns. Kids play in the playgrounds. The design makes you forget you're in the desert. This is part of the expo city dubai attractions that makes it worth visiting even without a specific event ticket.

What's New Since the Expo?

Expo City Dubai Arena

A new 10,000-seat arena opened in 2023. It hosts concerts, esports tournaments, and international events. Major artists have already performed here. The arena is designed to be one of the region's premier entertainment venues. This is the answer to expo city dubai what to do if you're looking for nightlife and events.

COP28 Legacy

Expo City Dubai hosted COP28, the UN Climate Change Conference, in late 2023. The infrastructure built for that event — conference halls, meeting spaces, and sustainability showcases — remains in use. The district is positioning itself as a hub for climate dialogue and green innovation. The dubai expo legacy is actively shaping global conversations.

New Hotels and Dining

The Rove Expo City hotel opened with Expo-themed design. New restaurants and cafes have launched across the district. You can spend a full day here now — breakfast, museums, a garden walk, dinner, and a show. It's becoming a genuine destination, not just a site to visit.

What's Coming Next?

Residential Communities

Expo City Dubai is building residential neighborhoods. The first apartments and townhouses are already under construction. The plan is to create a mixed-use community where people live, work, and play. This is long-term urban development, not just tourism.

More Pavilions Reopening

Additional country pavilions are being converted into permanent cultural and business spaces. Saudi Arabia's pavilion, one of the most visited during the Expo, is expected to become a major cultural center. The plan is to eventually have dozens of operational pavilions year-round.

Innovation and Business Hub

Expo City Dubai is positioning itself as a hub for startups, tech companies, and international organizations. The Dubai Expo 2020 legacy includes business districts, co-working spaces, and innovation labs. This is where the economic impact becomes real.

Why Visit Expo City Dubai?

Most people skip it because they think it's closed. It isn't. The expo city dubai attractions are diverse, affordable, and genuinely world-class. The architecture alone is worth the trip. The Sustainability Pavilion looks like a giant desert rock. The Mobility Pavilion resembles a giant silver eye. The UAE Pavilion is one of the most beautiful buildings in the country.

Plus, it's less crowded than Downtown Dubai. Parking is easier. The crowd is more local. You get incredible photos without fighting through tour groups. And many of the attractions are free or low-cost.

Practical Tips for Visitors

  • Getting there: Take the Dubai Metro Red Line to Expo Metro Station. It's the last stop and the station itself is impressive.
  • Best time to visit: October to April, like most outdoor activities in Dubai. Evenings are best for the light shows.
  • Tickets: Many outdoor areas are free. Individual pavilions charge separate entry fees. Check the official website for current prices.
  • How long to spend: Plan for at least half a day. A full day lets you explore multiple pavilions and catch the evening shows.
  • Food: Multiple dining options across the site, from quick bites to sit-down restaurants.

FAQ

Is Expo City Dubai still open to visitors?

Yes. Expo City Dubai opened to the public in October 2022 and remains open year-round. Multiple pavilions, parks, and attractions are operational. Some areas are free, while individual pavilions require tickets.

What happened to the country pavilions after Expo 2020?

Many pavilions closed, but several key ones — including the UAE Pavilion, Terra (Sustainability), Alif (Mobility), and Mission Possible (Opportunity) — remain open as museums and exhibition centers. More are being converted for permanent use.

How do I get to Expo City Dubai?

The easiest way is the Dubai Metro Red Line. Take the train to Expo Metro Station, which is the last stop. The station is directly connected to the site. There's also ample parking if you drive.

What are the main attractions at Expo City Dubai now?

The main attractions include Al Wasl Plaza with its dome projections, the Surreal water fountain, the Garden in the Sky observation tower, the Sustainability Pavilion (Terra), the Mobility Pavilion (Alif), and the UAE Pavilion. There's also a 10,000-seat arena for concerts and events.

Is Expo City Dubai worth visiting after the Expo ended?

Absolutely. The district has evolved into a permanent cultural and entertainment destination. The architecture is stunning, the museums are world-class, and it's less crowded than other Dubai attractions. It's especially worth visiting for the evening light shows and weekend events.
