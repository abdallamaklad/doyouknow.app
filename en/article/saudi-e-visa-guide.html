Saudi Visa System Overview

Saudi Arabia has undergone one of the most dramatic travel transformations in modern history. Since launching the Saudi e-Visa program in September 2019, the Kingdom has welcomed millions of international tourists, opened entertainment venues, and relaxed social restrictions that previously made independent travel nearly impossible.

Today, the Kingdom offers a streamlined digital visa system that covers tourism, business, pilgrimage, and family visits. Whether you are planning to explore the ancient Nabataean tombs of AlUla, attend a Formula 1 race in Jeddah, or perform Umrah in Makkah, understanding the correct Saudi visa type is essential for a smooth entry.

This guide covers every aspect of the Saudi visa system: e-Visa categories, eligible nationalities, step-by-step application instructions, fees, processing timelines, common rejection reasons, and special rules for Hajj, Umrah, and business travelers.

Saudi e-Visa Types

The Saudi e-Visa platform issues several visa categories depending on your purpose of travel. Here are the main types available to international visitors:

Tourism e-Visa

The most common Saudi e-Visa is the tourism visa, available as a single-entry or multiple-entry permit. It allows holders to visit Saudi Arabia for leisure, sightseeing, visiting friends and family, and attending events. The multiple-entry tourism visa is valid for one year and permits stays of up to 90 days per visit.

Event Visa

Saudi Arabia regularly hosts mega-events including Riyadh Season, MDL Beast music festivals, and international sporting events. The event visa is a short-term entry permit linked to specific ticketed events. It is typically valid for the duration of the event plus a short buffer period.

Stopover Visa

Introduced to boost transit tourism, the 96-hour stopover visa is free for passengers flying Saudia or Flynas with a connection in Saudi Arabia. It allows eligible travelers to leave the airport and explore the city during their layover. You must book the stopover through the airline's transit program.

Family Visit Visa

First-degree relatives of Saudi citizens or legal residents can apply for a family visit visa through the Absher or Muqeem platforms. This is not an e-Visa but a sponsored visa issued by the resident family member. It is valid for single or multiple entries depending on the sponsor's request.

Eligible Countries for e-Visa

The Saudi e-Visa is currently available to citizens of approximately 60 countries. The list is periodically updated by the Saudi Ministry of Foreign Affairs, so always verify eligibility before planning your trip.

Eligible regions include:

  • North America: United States, Canada
  • Europe: All EU/Schengen countries, United Kingdom, Switzerland, Norway, Iceland, Andorra, Monaco, San Marino, Liechtenstein, Montenegro, Albania, Russia
  • Asia-Pacific: Australia, New Zealand, Brunei, Japan, Singapore, Malaysia, South Korea, Kazakhstan, China, Hong Kong, Macao, Taiwan, India
  • Middle East & North Africa: Turkey, South Africa, Mauritius, Seychelles
  • Latin America: No e-Visa eligibility currently; apply via embassy

Indian citizens gained e-Visa eligibility in 2024, a significant expansion that reflects the Kingdom's growing tourism ambitions. Citizens of Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) countries do not need a visa to enter Saudi Arabia.

How to Apply for a Saudi e-Visa

The Saudi e-Visa application process is entirely online and typically takes less than 15 minutes to complete. Here is the exact step-by-step process:

Step 1: Visit the Official Portal

Go to the Visit Saudi portal or the Ministry of Foreign Affairs e-Visa platform. Third-party visa agencies are not required and often charge inflated fees.

Step 2: Create an Account

Register with a valid email address and mobile number. You will receive an OTP for verification. Use an email you can access easily, as all visa documents and updates are sent there.

Step 3: Fill the Application Form

Enter your personal details exactly as they appear in your passport. Upload a passport-sized photo with a white background and a clear scan of your passport's bio-data page. The passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your intended entry date.

Step 4: Select Visa Type and Insurance

Choose between single-entry or multiple-entry tourism visa. Travel insurance is mandatory and is bundled into the visa fee through approved Saudi providers. You cannot use external insurance for the e-Visa.

Step 5: Pay the Fee

Payment is accepted via credit or debit card. The total fee includes the visa cost, insurance, and a small processing charge. Save your receipt.

Step 6: Receive Your e-Visa

Most applicants receive their e-Visa by email within 5–30 minutes. In rare cases, it may take up to 24 hours if additional verification is needed. Print a copy and save a digital version on your phone.

Visa on Arrival

Citizens of the United States, United Kingdom, Schengen countries, and several other eligible nations can obtain a visa on arrival at Saudi international airports. This is functionally identical to the e-Visa but issued at the immigration counter.

Requirements at the airport:

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity)
  • Confirmed return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or host address)
  • Payment by credit card or cash (SAR 480 approximately)

The visa on arrival is available at King Khalid International Airport (Riyadh), King Abdulaziz International Airport (Jeddah), King Fahd International Airport (Dammam), and all other major international entry points. It is not available at land borders with GCC countries for most nationalities.

Business Visa

The Saudi business visa is for professionals attending meetings, conferences, contract negotiations, and short-term commercial activities. It is not a work permit: you cannot be employed by a Saudi entity on a business visa.

Application process: You need an invitation letter from a Saudi company registered with the Ministry of Investment (MISA) or the Chamber of Commerce. The Saudi sponsor initiates the application through the Enjaz platform. Once approved, the applicant completes the process at the nearest Saudi embassy or consulate.

Processing time: 3–10 working days depending on the sponsor's verification status and the embassy's workload. Some business visas are now available electronically for select nationalities through the simplified business visit visa program.

Hajj & Umrah Visa

Pilgrimage visas are distinct from tourism visas. The Umrah visa is available year-round, while the Hajj visa is issued only during the designated Hajj season and is allocated through official pilgrimage quotas to each country.

Umrah e-Visa: Pilgrims from eligible countries can now apply for an Umrah e-Visa through the Nusuk platform. The visa is valid for 30 days, with a maximum stay of 30 days. Women under 45 must be accompanied by a mahram (male guardian), though this rule has been relaxed for some nationalities in recent updates.

Hajj visa: You cannot apply for a Hajj visa independently. Each country has a designated number of Hajj quotas managed by the Ministry of Hajj and Umrah. Applications must go through approved travel agencies in your home country. Hajj visas are single-entry and expire shortly after the pilgrimage period ends.

Important note: Tourism visa holders can now perform Umrah without needing a separate Umrah visa. This is a major policy change introduced in 2023 that simplifies the process for millions of Muslim tourists.

Common Rejection Reasons

While the Saudi e-Visa approval rate is high for eligible nationalities, rejections do occur. Understanding the common causes can help you avoid a costly refusal:

  • Passport validity: Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months. Many applicants overlook this and are rejected immediately.
  • Previous overstay: If you have previously overstayed a Saudi visa or violated immigration rules, you may be flagged in the system.
  • Security concerns: The Saudi visa system cross-references international security databases. Any red flag will lead to rejection without explanation.
  • Photo non-compliance: Photos must be recent, with a white background, no glasses, and no head coverings unless for religious reasons with documentation.
  • Incorrect nationality selection: Some applicants hold dual citizenship. Selecting the wrong nationality or passport can trigger a mismatch.
  • Health restrictions: Certain health conditions or missing vaccination records (especially for Hajj/Umrah) can cause refusal.
  • Applying from a restricted country: Even if you hold an eligible passport, applying from a sanctioned or restricted country may complicate processing.

If your visa is rejected, the fee is not refunded. You can reapply after addressing the issue, but repeated applications without changes may result in a permanent ban.

Travel Tips for Saudi Arabia

Beyond the visa itself, a few practical tips will make your Saudi trip smoother:

  • Alcohol: Alcohol is strictly prohibited throughout the Kingdom. Do not attempt to bring alcohol through customs.
  • Dress code: Modest dress is required in public. Men should avoid shorts in government buildings. Women no longer need to wear an abaya but should dress modestly covering shoulders and knees in public spaces.
  • Photography: Do not photograph government buildings, military installations, or local people without consent.
  • Prayer times: Shops and restaurants close briefly during the five daily prayers. Plan shopping and dining accordingly.
  • Currency: The Saudi Riyal (SAR) is pegged to the US dollar at 3.75 SAR = 1 USD. Credit cards are widely accepted, but carry cash for rural areas and traditional souks.
  • Language: Arabic is the official language, but English is widely spoken in cities, hotels, and tourist venues.
  • Transport: Ride-hailing apps Uber and Careem operate extensively. Car rental is recommended for exploring regions like AlUla and the Asir Mountains.

FAQ

Who needs a visa to enter Saudi Arabia?

All foreign nationals except GCC citizens (UAE, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman) require a visa to enter Saudi Arabia. Citizens of approximately 60 eligible countries can apply for an e-Visa or obtain a visa on arrival. All other nationalities must apply through a Saudi embassy or consulate.

How much does a Saudi e-Visa cost?

A single-entry tourism e-Visa costs approximately SAR 535 (including mandatory insurance). A multiple-entry tourism e-Visa costs approximately SAR 1,075. The 96-hour stopover visa is free when booked through Saudia or Flynas. Fees are non-refundable.

How long does Saudi e-Visa processing take?

Most e-Visas are approved within 5 to 30 minutes. In rare cases with additional verification, it can take up to 24 hours. Business visas take 3–10 working days. Hajj visa processing is managed by approved agencies and follows seasonal timelines.

Can I extend my Saudi e-Visa?

Tourism e-Visas can be extended once for an additional 30 days through the Absher platform or by visiting a Jawazat office, provided you apply before the original expiry date. The extension fee is approximately SAR 100 plus any additional insurance costs.

Why was my Saudi e-Visa rejected?

Common rejection reasons include: passport validity under 6 months, previous immigration violations, security database flags, photo non-compliance, applying with a restricted nationality, or incorrect information on the application. Saudi authorities do not always disclose the exact reason for rejection.

Can I work in Saudi Arabia on a tourist visa?

No. Working on a tourist or business visa is illegal and punishable by fines, deportation, and a potential re-entry ban. To work legally, you must obtain an Iqama (residence permit) sponsored by a Saudi employer through the Ministry of Human Resources.

Can I perform Umrah on a Saudi tourism visa?

Yes. Since 2023, holders of Saudi tourism e-Visas can perform Umrah without needing a separate Umrah visa. You must book your Umrah appointment through the Nusuk app or platform. Hajj, however, still requires a dedicated Hajj visa arranged through an approved agency.

Do women need a male guardian to travel to Saudi Arabia?

Women over 18 can apply for a Saudi e-Visa independently and travel alone. For Umrah, women under 45 may still need a mahram (male guardian) depending on the latest Ministry of Hajj and Umrah regulations. Check the Nusuk platform for the most current rules before booking.

Is travel insurance mandatory for a Saudi visa?

Yes, travel insurance covering medical emergencies and COVID-19 treatment is mandatory for all e-Visa applicants. The insurance is automatically bundled into your visa fee and issued by an approved Saudi provider. External insurance policies are not accepted for the e-Visa application.

Can I get a Saudi visa on arrival?

Yes, citizens of the United States, United Kingdom, Schengen countries, and several other eligible nations can obtain a visa on arrival at major Saudi airports. The fee is approximately SAR 480, and you must have a valid passport, return ticket, and proof of accommodation. Visa on arrival is not available at land borders for most nationalities.
