Your Weekly Dubai Guide: What to Do This Week

Dubai moves fast. One week, a celebrity chef opens a rooftop restaurant. The next, a pop-up market takes over a warehouse in Al Quoz. Keeping track of what is happening — and what is actually worth your time — is a full-time job. That is why we update this guide every Monday with the best things to do in Dubai this week.

This is a living template. Each week, we refresh the specific events, venues, and times while keeping the structure the same. Bookmark this page. Share it with friends. Use it as your weekly ritual for planning a better Dubai.

Week of July 21-27, 2025

Monday: New Openings & Quiet Dining

Monday is the reset day. Most Dubai residents are recovering from the weekend, which means the city is quieter — and that is the advantage. Restaurants that are fully booked on Friday have tables available. Traffic is lighter. The beaches are emptier.

Dining: Monday is the best night to try the city's hardest-to-book restaurants. Tresind Studio at Nakheel Mall typically has last-minute availability on Mondays. The 16-course tasting menu is one of the most innovative in the city. For something more casual, 3 Fils in Jumeirah Fishing Harbour rarely has a wait on Monday evenings, and the wagyu sando is still the best-value premium dish in Dubai.

Culture: The Louvre Abu Dhabi (yes, it is worth the drive) is pleasantly uncrowded on Monday afternoons. The collection spans from ancient Egypt to contemporary Middle Eastern art, and the building itself — designed by Jean Nouvel — is worth the admission price alone. The drive from Dubai takes about 75 minutes.

Wellness: Many spas offer Monday discounts. Talise Spa at Madinat Jumeirah runs a 30% off "Monday Reset" deal on massages, and the setting — overlooking the water and the Burj Al Arab silhouette — is hard to beat.

Tuesday: Fitness & Wellness

Tuesday is when Dubai's fitness community comes alive. Whether you are a serious athlete or a casual gym-goer, the options are extensive.

Outdoor Fitness: The Dubai Fitness Challenge may only run in October, but the infrastructure it built remains. The Kite Beach outdoor gym is free, open 24 hours, and has pull-up bars, dip stations, and running tracks. The Al Qudra Cycling Track is 86 kilometers of smooth, car-free road through the desert. Sunrise rides (starting around 5:30 AM) are the most popular — bring water, even in winter.

Group Classes: Train Beach Club on Palm West Beach runs sunrise yoga every Tuesday at 7:00 AM, followed by a healthy breakfast. The class is donation-based, and the beach setting makes it feel like a vacation, not a workout. Warehouse Gym locations across the city offer free trial classes on Tuesdays — everything from HIIT to boxing.

Recovery: Ice baths and contrast therapy are trending in Dubai. Restore in DIFC offers guided ice bath sessions on Tuesday evenings, and the mental clarity afterward is genuinely noticeable. Book ahead — spots are limited to 8 people.

Wednesday: Ladies' Night & Midweek Brunches

Wednesday is unofficially the start of the weekend in Dubai. Ladies' night deals are city-wide, and several venues extend them to include food as well as drinks.

Ladies' Night: Barasti Beach (Le Meridien Mina Seyahi) offers free drinks for women from 8 PM to midnight every Wednesday. The beachfront setting is casual, the crowd is mixed, and the sunset views are excellent. Miss Lily's at Sheraton Grand Hotel runs a Jamaican-themed ladies' night with free cocktails and 50% off food. For a more upscale option, Atelier M at Pier 7 offers three free drinks and 30% off dining on the rooftop terrace.

Midweek Brunches: While Friday is the classic brunch day, several venues now offer Wednesday versions at lower prices. The Penthouse at FIVE Palm Jumeirah runs a "Wednesdays at The Penthouse" package with unlimited sushi and drinks for 250 AED. The views over the Palm are exceptional, and the crowd is more relaxed than on weekends.

Live Music: Hard Rock Cafe on the Dubai Fountain promenade hosts an open-mic night every Wednesday. The talent level is surprisingly high, and the atmosphere is welcoming for both performers and listeners.

Thursday: Concerts & Live Music

Thursday is the peak night for entertainment in Dubai. Major concerts, comedy shows, and live performances are scheduled for this evening because it is the start of the weekend for most residents.

Concerts: This week, Coca-Cola Arena hosts a regional Arabic pop artist (check the venue's website for the specific act, as Thursday concerts rotate weekly). The arena is Dubai's premier indoor venue, with capacity for 17,000 and excellent acoustics. Blu Dubai at the Wyndham hosts Thursday R&B nights with live bands and DJs. For electronic music, SoBe at W Dubai — The Palm brings in international DJs on Thursday nights.

Comedy: The Laughter Factory runs weekly comedy shows at rotating venues across the city. Thursday is their headline night, typically featuring an international touring comedian. Tickets are around 150 AED and often sell out. Dubai Comedy Festival events (when running) also cluster on Thursday and Friday evenings.

Live Jazz: Jazz@PizzaExpress in JLT and DIFC hosts live jazz every Thursday. The DIFC location is more upscale; the JLT version is more relaxed. Both are free entry, though you should book a table for dinner.

Friday: Beach Clubs & Pool Parties

Friday is the ultimate beach day in Dubai. The weather is typically perfect, and the city's beach clubs pull out all the stops.

Beach Clubs: Zero Gravity near Dubai Marina is the classic Friday pool party. Entry is around 200 AED (fully redeemable on food and drink), and the atmosphere is high-energy without being overwhelming. Drift Beach Club at One&Only Royal Mirage is the sophisticated alternative — infinity pool, direct beach access, and a restaurant that serves some of the best salads in Dubai. Day passes start at 350 AED on Fridays.

Family Beach: Not every Friday needs to be a party. Kite Beach is free, has volleyball courts, a skate park, and a wide selection of food trucks. La Mer (now rebranded as J1 Beach in parts) offers a more curated beach experience with retail and dining along the boardwalk. The water park at Laguna Waterpark in La Mer is 50% off on Fridays before noon.

Brunch Recovery: After a big Friday brunch, sunset dhow cruises from Dubai Marina offer a gentle way to ease into the evening. The two-hour cruises include dinner and pass the Atlantis, the Palm, and the Burj Al Arab. Prices start at 120 AED.

Saturday: Markets & Family Activities

Saturday is family day in Dubai. The work week is over, schools are closed, and the city is in full leisure mode.

Markets: The Ripe Market at Dubai Police Academy Park runs every Saturday morning from 9 AM to 2 PM. It is Dubai's best farmers' market, with organic produce, artisanal bread, handmade crafts, and live cooking demos. The atmosphere is community-oriented, and it is one of the few places in Dubai where you will see more locals than tourists. Alserkal Avenue in Al Quoz occasionally hosts Saturday art markets — check their Instagram for the current schedule.

Family Activities: Dubai Miracle Garden is open (seasonally, typically November to May) and Saturday mornings are the least crowded time to visit. The floral displays are genuinely impressive, and children love the Disney-themed sections. Dubai Aquarium at Dubai Mall offers Saturday morning "behind the scenes" tours where kids can feed the fish and learn about marine conservation. Deep Dive Dubai, the world's deepest swimming pool, runs family-friendly discovery sessions on Saturdays.

Evening: Dubai Fountain shows run every 30 minutes from 6 PM to 11 PM on Saturdays. The 6:30 PM and 7:00 PM shows are the least crowded. Combine with dinner at one of the restaurants on the Dubai Mall promenade for a full evening out.

Sunday: Free Activities & Wind Down

Sunday is the quietest day of the week in Dubai. Many venues are closed, and residents are preparing for the work week. But that makes it ideal for free, low-key activities.

Free Cultural Sites: Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood in Bur Dubai is free to explore. The narrow alleyways, wind towers, and courtyard houses offer a glimpse of Dubai before the oil boom. The Sheikh Mohammed Centre for Cultural Understanding runs cultural breakfasts and tours — some are free, others are donation-based. The Dubai Museum (when reopened after renovation) is the most comprehensive overview of the city's history.

Beach: Sunset Beach (near Umm Suqeim) is free and offers the most iconic view of the Burj Al Arab. Sunday evenings are particularly peaceful — the weekend crowds have gone, and the sunset light is perfect for photography. Bring a picnic and watch the sky turn pink behind the sail-shaped hotel.

Free Fitness: The Dubai Running Track along the Marina promenade is free and open 24 hours. Sunday morning (6-8 AM) is the best time — the temperature is cool, the light is golden, and the crowd is serious but friendly. RTA also offers free fitness classes at select metro stations on Sunday mornings.

Window Shopping: Even if you are not buying, Dubai Mall and Mall of the Emirates are entertainment destinations in their own right. The aquarium, the indoor ski slope, and the fountain shows are all free to watch. Sunday afternoon is the least crowded time to stroll.

Weekly Brunch Guide

Dubai's brunch culture is legendary. The Friday feast is practically a religion, but Saturday brunches are increasingly popular for those who want to pace themselves. Here is the current lineup:

Ultra-Luxury (500+ AED): Al Qasr at Madinat Jumeirah is the most famous Friday brunch in Dubai. Seven restaurants, live cooking stations, unlimited premium drinks, and a setting that looks like a palace. Traiteur at Park Hyatt is the sophisticated alternative — French cuisine, live jazz, and a terrace overlooking the creek.

Mid-Range (250-400 AED): Yalumba at Le Meridien offers Australian-Asian fusion with a strong seafood selection. Drift Beach Club does a beachside brunch that feels more like a vacation than a meal. Miss Tess at Taj Dubai is the best Asian street-food brunch in the city.

Budget (100-200 AED): McGettigan's does a no-nonsense Irish brunch with hearty food and live sports. The Irish Village is the classic pub brunch — big portions, reasonable prices, and an outdoor garden. Operation: Falafel runs a plant-based brunch that is genuinely excellent, even for meat-eaters.

Pro tip: Book at least two weeks in advance for the top-tier brunches. Many venues offer early-bird discounts for bookings made 30 days ahead.

Free Events This Week

Dubai can be expensive, but it does not have to be. Here are the best free things to do this week:

Dubai Fountain Show: Every evening, every 30 minutes, completely free. The 6 PM show is the most family-friendly; the 9 PM show is the most dramatic. The waterfront promenade at Dubai Mall offers the best view, but the bridge between Dubai Mall and Souk Al Bahar is less crowded.

Al Qudra Sunrise: The cycling track is free to use, and the sunrise over the desert is one of the most beautiful sights in the UAE. You do not need a bike — the walking paths are equally impressive. Bring coffee and watch the sun turn the sand dunes gold.

Art Nights at Alserkal: On select evenings, Alserkal Avenue galleries open late and offer free entry. Check Alserkal Avenue's website for the current schedule. Evenings typically include artist talks, live performances, and free refreshments.

Beach Sunset at Kite Beach: Free entry, free parking (if you arrive before 4 PM), and some of the best people-watching in the city. The sunset over the Arabian Gulf is reliable and beautiful.

Public Library Events: Mohammed Bin Rashid Library runs free lectures, book signings, and children's workshops throughout the week. The building itself is architecturally stunning, and the view from the top floor is worth the trip alone.

New Openings

Dubai's restaurant and nightlife scene changes constantly. Here is what is new this month:

Restaurants: A new Japanese omakase counter has opened in DIFC, offering 12 seats and a 20-course tasting menu focused on Hokkaido seafood. A Levantine rooftop has launched in JBR with views over the Marina and a menu that reimagines classic Levantine dishes with modern techniques. In Al Quoz, a new food hall has converted a warehouse into a market with 12 vendors, focusing on local and sustainable produce.

Nightlife: A new speakeasy-style cocktail bar has opened behind an unmarked door in Downtown Dubai. The drinks menu is designed by a former London World Class finalist, and the reservation-only policy keeps it intimate. On the Palm, a new beach club has opened with a focus on wellness — morning yoga, cold plunge pools, and organic food rather than loud DJs and bottle service.

Experiences: The new Dubai Trolley (a heritage tram) has extended its route through more of Downtown, offering a scenic (and air-conditioned) way to see the Burj Khalifa district. Hatta has added new mountain bike trails and a zipline, making it the best outdoor adventure destination within 90 minutes of the city.

FAQ

When is the best night to go out in Dubai?

Thursday is the busiest night for clubs and concerts, while Wednesday is best for ladies' nights and midweek deals. Friday is peak for brunches and beach clubs. Saturday is ideal for family-friendly activities. Sunday and Monday are the quietest nights, which is perfect if you prefer a relaxed atmosphere.

Do I need to book brunches in advance?

Yes, especially for popular Friday brunches at top hotels. Book 2-3 weeks ahead for ultra-luxury venues like Al Qasr or Traiteur. Mid-range brunches can often be booked a few days in advance, but Friday slots still fill up quickly. Some venues offer early-bird discounts for bookings made 30 days ahead.

Are beach clubs in Dubai family-friendly?

Some are, some are not. Zero Gravity and Drift Beach Club are adults-oriented on Fridays but allow children on Saturdays. Kite Beach and La Mer are fully family-friendly every day. Most hotel beach clubs have a family section separate from the party area. Check the venue's policy before booking.

What time do Dubai clubs close?

Most clubs and bars close at 3 AM, with last entry typically around 1:30 AM. Some venues in licensed hotels can extend later on weekends. During Ramadan and public holidays, hours may be restricted. Dubai does not have a 24-hour club license, so the 3 AM cutoff is standard.

What is the dress code for Dubai nightlife?

Smart casual is the minimum for most venues. Beach clubs accept swimwear during the day but require cover-ups for indoor areas. Upscale lounges and rooftop bars typically require smart elegant attire — no shorts, flip-flops, or sportswear for men. Ladies' night venues are generally more relaxed.
