How Is the Islamic New Year Celebrated?

What Is the Islamic New Year?

The Islamic New Year, also called the Hijri New Year, marks the beginning of a new year in the Islamic lunar calendar. It begins on the first day of Muharram, the first month of the Hijri year.

Unlike the Gregorian New Year on 1 January, the Islamic New Year moves backward through the solar year by about ten or eleven days annually. This is because the Islamic calendar follows the moon rather than the sun.

The Hijrah: Why the Calendar Begins Here

The Islamic calendar begins with the Hijrah, the migration of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) and the early Muslims from Mecca to Medina in 622 CE. This journey transformed a persecuted community into the foundation of a new society.

The caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab later fixed the calendar to start from the year of the Hijrah, making it a permanent reminder of faith, planning, and sacrifice.

Muharram: The Sacred Month

Muharram is one of the four sacred months in Islam. Fighting was traditionally forbidden during these months, and Muslims are encouraged to increase good deeds and avoid sin.

The tenth day of Muharram is Ashura. Sunni Muslims fast on this day as a recommended Sunnah, while Shia Muslims commemorate the martyrdom of Imam Hussein at Karbala.

How the New Year Is Observed

There are no prescribed communal prayers for the Islamic New Year, but many Muslims use the day for reflection, gratitude, and setting spiritual goals. Families may gather for special meals, visit relatives, and exchange greetings.

Charity is especially encouraged at the start of Muharram. Some Muslims choose to pay Zakat or give Sadaqah as the year begins. Learn more about charity in our guide to Sadaqah vs Zakat.

The Calendar and Worship

The Hijri calendar shapes the timing of Ramadan, Hajj, and Eid. Because it is lunar, these occasions move through the seasons over a 33-year cycle.

Knowing when Muharram begins helps Muslims prepare for Ashura and plan the spiritual year ahead. Our Hijri calendar guide explains the lunar system in detail.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Islamic New Year called?

It is called the Hijri New Year or Ras as-Sanah al-Hijriyah.

Why does the date change every year?

The Islamic calendar is lunar, so it is about eleven days shorter than the solar year.

Is the Islamic New Year a public holiday?

In many Muslim-majority countries, including Saudi Arabia and the UAE, it is an official public holiday.

What is Ashura?

Ashura is the 10th day of Muharram. Sunni Muslims fast it as a Sunnah; Shia Muslims mourn Imam Hussein.

How can I observe the Islamic New Year?

Reflect on the past year, set spiritual goals, read about the Hijrah, give charity, and spend time with family.
