The Two Holy Mosques

The Two Holy Mosques are Masjid al-Haram in Mecca and Masjid an-Nabawi in Medina. They are the most sacred mosques in Islam and the destinations of Hajj, Umrah, and visitation.

Together they connect the beginnings of Islam: the Kaaba, built by Prophet Ibrahim and his son Ismail, and the Prophet's Mosque, established by Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

Masjid al-Haram and the Kaaba

Masjid al-Haram surrounds the Kaaba, the first house of worship built for humanity. Muslims around the world face the Kaaba during their five daily prayers.

According to Islamic tradition, the Kaaba was built by Prophet Ibrahim and his son Ismail thousands of years ago. It has been rebuilt several times throughout history, most recently in the modern era.

Masjid an-Nabawi: The Prophet's Mosque

Masjid an-Nabawi was built by the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) after his migration to Medina. It was originally a simple structure with palm trunks and a roof of palm leaves.

Over the centuries it has grown into one of the largest mosques in the world. It contains the Rawdah, a garden area described as one of the gardens of Paradise, and the Prophet's burial chamber.

Expansion and Modern Care

Both mosques have undergone massive expansions, especially under the custodianship of Saudi Arabia. The latest expansions added capacity for millions of worshippers, improved accessibility, and introduced advanced climate control and transport systems.

These projects aim to serve the growing number of pilgrims and visitors while preserving the sanctity and historical identity of each mosque.

Spiritual Significance

Prayer in Masjid al-Haram is multiplied in reward, and prayer in Masjid an-Nabawi is also highly rewarded. The Kaaba symbolizes unity, as all Muslims face it regardless of nationality or background.

Visiting these mosques is part of Hajj and Umrah. Our Hajj guide and Umrah guide explain how to prepare.

Visiting the Two Holy Mosques Today

Millions of Muslims visit the Two Holy Mosques each year. Pilgrims need valid visas, travel arrangements, and knowledge of mosque etiquette. The Saudi Ministry of Hajj and Umrah provides official guidance and services.

Visitors are reminded to respect the sacred space, maintain patience in crowds, and focus on worship and reflection.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the Two Holy Mosques?

Masjid al-Haram in Mecca and Masjid an-Nabawi in Medina.

Who built the Kaaba?

According to Islamic tradition, the Kaaba was built by Prophet Ibrahim and his son Ismail.

Can non-Muslims enter the Two Holy Mosques?

No, only Muslims are permitted to enter the city of Mecca and the mosques.

What is the Rawdah?

It is a blessed area inside Masjid an-Nabawi, described as one of the gardens of Paradise.

How are the mosques managed today?

They are under the custodianship of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, which oversees maintenance, expansion, and services.
