UAE Golden Visa Apply: The Complete 2026 Guide

You can get a 10-year UAE residency visa without a local sponsor, without a job, and without ever needing to renew it every two years. That is not a sales pitch. That is the UAE Golden Visa program, and if you are serious about long-term life in the Emirates, you need to understand how it actually works—not the brochure version.

This guide covers uae golden visa apply procedures from start to finish. We will break down uae golden visa requirements, real uae golden visa cost figures, and the exact steps you need to take. Whether you are an investor, a professional, a student, or an entrepreneur, there is a path for you. By the end of this uae golden visa apply guide, you will know exactly what documents you need, how much to budget, and which portal to use.

What Is the UAE Golden Visa?

The UAE Golden Visa is a long-term residence visa that allows foreign nationals to live, work, and study in the UAE for up to 10 years without needing a national sponsor. It is renewable. It allows you to sponsor your family members, including spouse and children. And it gives you the right to stay outside the UAE for more than six months without losing your visa status—a flexibility normal residence visas do not offer.

Introduced in 2019 and expanded significantly in 2021 and 2022, the program now covers more categories than ever before. The uae golden visa 2026 framework continues to evolve, with new professional categories and streamlined application processes added regularly.

Who Is Eligible? UAE Golden Visa Requirements

Uae golden visa requirements vary by category. The UAE government has created distinct pathways for different types of applicants. Here is the full breakdown.

Investors

Real estate investors must own property or properties in the UAE with a total value of at least AED 2 million (approximately USD 545,000). The property can be mortgaged, but only the equity portion paid by the investor counts toward the threshold. Off-plan properties are acceptable if they are from approved developers.

Business investors must have an investment of at least AED 2 million in a UAE-registered company, or be a partner in a business with that capital value. Some free zones have their own specific thresholds, so check with your free zone authority.

Entrepreneurs

Entrepreneurs qualify if they are a founder or co-founder of a startup registered in the UAE with a minimum valuation of AED 1 million, or if they have a project approved by an accredited business incubator. They can also qualify if they have founded a business that generates annual revenues of at least AED 1 million.

Specialized Talents and Professionals

This is the broadest and most expanded category. Uae golden visa requirements for professionals include:

  • Doctors and medical professionals: Must hold a license from the UAE's health regulatory authority.
  • Scientists: Must have a PhD from a top-tier university and substantial research contributions.
  • Engineers: Must hold a degree in a specialized engineering field from an accredited university.
  • IT professionals and programmers: Must hold a degree or certification in software, data science, AI, or related fields.
  • Creative and cultural professionals: Artists, designers, and cultural specialists recognized by relevant UAE authorities.
  • Executive directors and specialists: Must hold a bachelor's degree, earn a minimum salary of AED 30,000 per month, and have at least 5 years of experience in a leadership role.
  • Legal and financial professionals: Lawyers, accountants, and auditors with recognized qualifications and experience.
  • Athletes and sports professionals: Recognized by UAE sports authorities.
  • PhD holders: Any PhD from a top 500 global university qualifies, regardless of field.
  • University graduates: Outstanding graduates from UAE universities or the top 100 global universities.

Outstanding Students

High school students with top grades in UAE schools, and university graduates with distinction from the top 100 global universities, can apply. The age threshold for student categories is typically under 30.

Frontline Heroes

Healthcare workers and other frontline professionals who demonstrated exceptional service during the COVID-19 pandemic were granted Golden Visas as a recognition program. This category is now largely closed to new applicants but remains valid for existing holders.

How to Get Golden Visa UAE: The Step-by-Step Process

How to get golden visa uae procedures differ slightly depending on whether you are applying through Dubai or the other emirates (federal system). Here is the standard process.

Step 1: Determine Your Category

Before you spend a single dirham, confirm which category you qualify under. Visit the Federal Authority for Identity and Citizenship (ICP) website or the GDRFA Dubai portal to review the current eligibility list. Requirements change.

Step 2: Gather Your Documents

You will need: passport copy, photographs, Emirates ID (if resident), attested degrees, professional licenses, employment contract or salary certificate, property title deed (for investors), health insurance, and a good conduct certificate (for some categories). All foreign documents must be attested by your home country, the UAE embassy there, and the UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

Step 3: Submit Your Application Online

  • For Dubai residents: Apply through the GDRFA Dubai portal or app.
  • For other emirates: Apply through the ICP Smart Services portal.
  • For new applicants outside the UAE: You can apply through the same portals, but you will need to enter the UAE to complete medical testing and biometrics.

Step 4: Pay the Fees

Uae golden visa cost varies by category and emirate, but here is the general range for Golden Visa Dubai and federal applications:

| Item | Cost (AED) | Notes |

|------|-----------|-------|

| Application fee | 3,000 - 4,000 | Base processing fee |

| Medical fitness test | 250 - 750 | Required for all applicants |

| Emirates ID | 370 - 1,170 | 10-year ID cost |

| Health insurance | 1,500 - 5,000+ | Varies by age and coverage |

| Residency stamping | 500 - 1,000 | On passport or digital |

| Typing center service | 100 - 300 | Optional but common |

| Total estimated | 5,000 - 12,000 | Plus insurance and attestation costs |

Investor categories may have additional property registration or business licensing fees. Professional categories may need to pay for degree attestation, which can cost AED 1,000-3,000 per document depending on your country of origin.

Step 5: Complete Medical Testing

All applicants must pass a medical fitness test at an approved health center in the UAE. This includes a blood test for communicable diseases and a chest X-ray. The test is quick—usually 30 minutes—and results are typically available within 24-48 hours.

Step 6: Biometrics and Emirates ID

Visit an ICP or GDRFA biometric center for fingerprinting and a photo. Your 10-year Emirates ID will be issued after this step. The ID card itself takes about 5-7 working days to print, but your digital ID is active immediately.

Step 7: Receive Your Golden Visa

Once approved, your Golden Visa is stamped in your passport or issued digitally. The processing time is typically 2 to 4 weeks from application to approval, assuming no document issues. Some applications are approved in under 10 days.

Golden Visa Dubai vs. Federal Applications

Golden visa dubai applications are processed by the General Directorate of Residency and Foreigners Affairs (GDRFA). Other emirates use the Federal Identity and Citizenship Authority (ICP). The requirements are similar, but the fees and processing speed differ slightly. Dubai's system is often faster for digital applications, but ICP has broader category coverage for specialized professionals.

If you are applying as a property investor in Dubai, your property must be registered with the Dubai Land Department. The GDRFA system will cross-check the title deed automatically.

UAE Golden Visa 2026: What Has Changed Recently

The uae golden visa 2026 landscape includes several recent updates:

  • Expanded professional categories: More skilled professionals now qualify, including data scientists, AI specialists, and cybersecurity experts.
  • Reduced salary thresholds: Some categories saw the minimum salary requirement lowered from AED 50,000 to AED 30,000 per month.
  • Simplified student pathways: Outstanding graduates from a wider range of universities now qualify.
  • Family sponsorship rules: Golden Visa holders can now sponsor parents more easily, and there is no cap on the number of domestic workers.
  • Remote work flexibility: Golden Visa holders can work remotely for foreign companies while residing in the UAE, provided they meet certain tax and registration conditions.

Benefits of the Golden Visa

Beyond the 10-year duration, the Golden Visa offers: no local sponsor required, extended stay outside the UAE without losing status, family sponsorship for spouse, children, and parents, unlimited domestic helpers, 100% foreign ownership of mainland businesses, and a clear path to UAE tax residency. Golden Visa holders can also sponsor entry visas for associates in some cases.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Assuming your degree is enough: Your degree must be attested by the UAE Ministry of Education. A foreign transcript alone is not sufficient.
  • Ignoring the salary certificate: For professional categories, your salary must be clearly documented on an official company letterhead with attestation.
  • Buying property that doesn't qualify: Off-plan properties from non-approved developers may not count toward the AED 2 million threshold. Verify the developer's status with the DLD or relevant land department.
  • Skipping health insurance: You cannot complete the visa process without valid UAE health insurance.
  • Waiting too long: Document attestation can take weeks. Start early.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I apply for the UAE Golden Visa from outside the country?

Yes, you can submit the initial application online. However, you must enter the UAE to complete medical testing and biometrics before the visa is finalized. You can do this on a standard entry visa or e-visa.

Q: How long does the UAE Golden Visa application take?

Typically 2 to 4 weeks from a complete application. Some applications are approved in 7-10 days if all documents are in order and the category is straightforward. Delays usually happen due to document attestation issues or missing medical tests.

Q: Can I lose my Golden Visa if I lose my job?

No. The Golden Visa is not tied to employment. That is the entire point. Even if you leave your job, close your business, or sell your property (in some cases), you may retain your visa depending on the category rules. Property investors must maintain the AED 2 million property value to renew.

Q: What is the minimum salary for a professional Golden Visa?

The general minimum is AED 30,000 per month for executive directors and specialists, though some categories have different thresholds. Check the current requirements on the GDRFA or ICP portal, as these are updated periodically.

Q: Can I sponsor my parents on a Golden Visa?

Yes, Golden Visa holders can sponsor parents, but the requirements are stricter than for spouse and children. You typically need to demonstrate higher income and provide suitable accommodation. The exact rules vary between Dubai and federal systems.
