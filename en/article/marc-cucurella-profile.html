Marc Cucurella: Profile of Spain's Left-Back Ahead of 2026

Who Is Marc Cucurella?

Marc Cucurella is Spain's first-choice left-back and one of the Premier League's most recognisable defenders — the curly-haired Chelsea player who went from being questioned by his own supporters to assisting the winning goal in a European Championship final. As World Cup 2026 approaches, he is central to the plans of the reigning European champions.

This profile traces his route from Barcelona's academy to Stamford Bridge, the playing style that made him indispensable, and what his role is likely to be as Spain head to North America for the 2026 finals.

At 27, he is also entering what defenders call the prime years — old enough to read the game, young enough to cover the flank for 90 minutes twice a week.

Along the way we also look at the numbers and honours that frame his standing among Europe's left-backs.

Career Path: La Masia to Stamford Bridge

Born on 22 July 1998 in Alella, Catalonia, Cucurella came through Barcelona's La Masia academy and made his senior debut for the club in a Copa del Rey tie in 2018. Seeking minutes, he rebuilt his career on loan: a season at Eibar in 2018–19, then a move to Getafe that became permanent in 2020. His energy and left foot made him one of La Liga's standout full-backs.

Brighton brought him to England in 2021, where a single impressive season earned him a big-money move to Chelsea in August 2022 — reported at the time as a package rising to around £62 million. His first year in London was difficult, with form and confidence suffering, but from the second half of the 2023–24 season he re-established himself, and under successive managers he has become a near-automatic starter. As of publication, his honours with Chelsea include the UEFA Conference League and the FIFA Club World Cup, both won in 2025.

Internationally, his route was just as unusual: his senior Spain debut came in June 2021 in a one-off fixture played by the under-21 squad during a COVID-19 isolation period, and he then spent years outside the selection. Recalled in 2024, he seized the left-back position at Euro 2024 — and never gave it back.

At Getafe, his first permanent home in senior football, he played every kind of match a full-back can: relegation fights, cup upsets, European nights. Those two seasons turned a talented academy graduate into a defender who expected contact and thrived on it.

Playing Style: The Modern Inverted Full-Back

Cucurella is a left-back who plays like a midfielder with a defender's appetite for contact. At Chelsea and with Spain he frequently steps into central positions when his team builds up — the inverted role — while remaining one of the most aggressive duel-winners in his position. His combination of ball security and edge makes him unusually flexible: he can overlap like a traditional full-back or tuck in as an auxiliary pivot.

That versatility had its defining showcase at Euro 2024, where his partnership with winger Nico Williams drove Spain's left flank. Williams attacked with freedom because Cucurella covered and underlapped behind him; in the final against England, it was Cucurella's cross that Mikel Oyarzabal turned in for the 2–1 winner. For a player whose move to England was once labelled a flop, it was a complete vindication.

Off the pitch, coaches describe him as a low-maintenance professional: durable, tactically obedient and comfortable in high-pressure matches. Those traits matter in tournament football, where managers prize players who execute a plan without drama.

Honours and Key Numbers

The trophy cabinet tells the story of a late bloomer. With Spain, Cucurella owns a European Championship (2024) and an Olympic silver medal from Tokyo 2020. With Chelsea, the 2024–25 season delivered the UEFA Conference League title and, weeks later, the FIFA Club World Cup — the first edition of the expanded 32-team tournament. Earlier in his career, a Copa del Rey medal with Barcelona and promotion battles with Getafe shaped his education.

Statistically, his profile is that of a high-volume full-back: among the league leaders for touches and duels at his position in recent Premier League seasons, with a goals-and-assists record that improved as his attacking role grew. He is rarely the headline act — but coaches build structures around players like him precisely because the structure holds.

Individually, his Euro 2024 displays earned him a place in many analysts' teams of the tournament.

Spain's Left-Back and the Road to World Cup 2026

Spain arrive at World Cup 2026 as European champions and one of the tournament favourites, and left-back is one of their deepest positions: Cucurella competes with the likes of Alejandro Balde and Álex Grimaldo, depending on fitness and form. As of mid-2026, Cucurella's experience and his Euro 2024 credit with the coaching staff keep him at the front of that queue — though final squad selection always rests with the coach.

His role, if selected as starter, is predictable in the best sense: aggressive coverage of the left channel, underlapping support for the winger, and secure first-phase build-up against the high presses expected from South American and European opponents. Spain's system leans on full-backs who can play inside as well as outside, which is precisely his profile.

For Arab football fans, Spain's group-stage path may cross Arab opposition — and Cucurella's duel with whichever right winger stands opposite him will be one of the quiet battles that decides those matches. Watch the space behind him as much as the ball: how Spain manage that channel tells you how far they expect to go.

FAQ

Which club does Marc Cucurella play for?

Chelsea, where he has played since August 2022. He wears the number 3 shirt and operates mainly as a left-back or wing-back.

What is Marc Cucurella's playing position?

Left-back, with the versatility to play wing-back or step into midfield as an inverted full-back — a role he performs for both Chelsea and Spain.

Did Cucurella win Euro 2024 with Spain?

Yes. He started the final against England in Berlin and delivered the cross for Mikel Oyarzabal's winning goal in the 2–1 victory, completing a standout tournament.

Will Cucurella play at World Cup 2026?

He is widely expected to be in Spain's squad and contends for the starting left-back role, but squad selection is the coach's decision and depends on form and fitness through the 2025–26 season.

How much did Chelsea pay for Cucurella?

The August 2022 transfer from Brighton was widely reported as a package rising to around £62 million, including add-ons. Chelsea did not publish an official fee.
