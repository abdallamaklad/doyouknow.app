What Happens During Laylat al-Qadr?

What Is Laylat al-Qadr?

Laylat al-Qadr, the Night of Decree, is the most blessed night of the year. It is the night on which the Qur'an was first revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

Allah describes it in the Qur'an as "better than a thousand months." Worship on this single night is worth more than a lifetime of ordinary worship.

When Does Laylat al-Qadr Occur?

The exact date is not known with certainty. It falls on one of the odd-numbered nights during the last ten nights of Ramadan: the 21st, 23rd, 25th, 27th, or 29th night.

Many scholars consider the 27th night of Ramadan the most likely, but the Prophet (peace be upon him) encouraged seeking it throughout the last ten nights so that Muslims would not miss it.

Signs of Laylat al-Qadr

The Prophet described several signs. The night is calm and pleasant, not too hot or too cold. The sun rises the next morning without strong rays, appearing like a disk, because of the angels ascending at dawn.

Some people experience a sense of peace, concentration in prayer, or tears during supplication. These are personal experiences, not guaranteed proofs.

What Muslims Do on Laylat al-Qadr

Muslims spend the night in prayer, Qur'an recitation, dhikr, and dua. They ask for forgiveness, guidance, and blessings for themselves and others. Charity given on this night is especially encouraged.

Many mosques hold special prayers and programs. Families may stay awake together, share meals before the pre-dawn meal, and make a list of sincere supplications.

Why the Night Is So Rewarding

On Laylat al-Qadr, Allah decrees the affairs of the coming year and showers mercy on His servants. The angels descend with permission of their Lord, and peace prevails until dawn.

Because its exact night is hidden, the mercy is available to anyone who sincerely seeks it. This hidden nature teaches humility and persistent effort.

Common Misconceptions

Laylat al-Qadr is not a festival with rituals like Eid. It does not require a specific prayer format. It is a night of personal devotion, and its value comes from sincere connection with Allah.

It is also not limited to a specific country or mosque. Muslims can seek it at home, in the mosque, or wherever they are able to worship.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does Laylat al-Qadr mean?

It means the Night of Decree or Night of Power, when the Qur'an began to be revealed.

Which night is Laylat al-Qadr?

It is one of the odd nights in the last ten nights of Ramadan, most commonly believed to be the 27th.

What should I do on Laylat al-Qadr?

Pray, recite Qur'an, make dhikr, give charity, and ask Allah for forgiveness and guidance.

Is Laylat al-Qadr only for adults?

No, people of all ages can seek its blessings through age-appropriate worship and good deeds.

Can women seek Laylat al-Qadr while menstruating?

They cannot pray or fast, but they can make dhikr, dua, give charity, and reflect on the Qur'an.
