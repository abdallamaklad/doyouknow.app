Saudi Mobile Plans: Compare STC, Mobily, Zain, Virgin

doyouknow.app Editorial Team2026-07-1014 min read

Editorial note: This guide was checked against official Saudi government sources and service portals on 2026-07-10. Fees, procedures, and eligibility rules can change, so confirm details through the official channels linked below before acting.

The Saudi Mobile Market

Saudi Arabia has one of the most advanced mobile markets in the Middle East. Four major operators compete for customers: STC, Mobily, Zain, and Virgin Mobile Saudi Arabia. All four offer 4G and 5G services, extensive coverage, and a variety of prepaid and postpaid plans. Choosing the right plan depends on your budget, data needs, and whether you need local calls or international minutes.

The Communications, Space and Technology Commission regulates the sector and sets rules on pricing, coverage, and consumer protection. Number portability is available, which means you can keep your phone number when switching between operators. This competition benefits consumers through better prices and regular promotions.

Mobile connectivity is essential for daily life in Saudi Arabia. Residents use their phones for banking through SADAD, government services through Absher, ride-hailing, food delivery, navigation, and staying in touch with family. This guide explains how to compare plans and avoid common mistakes.

The Four Main Operators

STC is the largest operator and has the widest 5G network. It offers a broad range of prepaid and postpaid plans, family packages, and bundled entertainment services such as Jawwy TV. STC is a safe choice if you travel frequently within Saudi Arabia because its rural coverage is generally the strongest.

Mobily is the second-largest operator and competes aggressively on price. It offers flexible prepaid bundles and postpaid plans with generous data allowances. Mobily’s network is strong in cities and along major highways. It is a popular choice for expatriates who want good value for money.

Zain offers competitive data packages and attractive international calling options. It has invested heavily in 5G and has good coverage in urban areas. Zain also provides special plans for youth and families. If you make many international calls, Zain is worth comparing.

Virgin Mobile Saudi Arabia is a digital-first mobile virtual network operator. It runs on STC’s network and offers app-based plans with no branches to visit. Virgin is popular among younger users and those who prefer managing everything through a mobile app.

Prepaid vs Postpaid Plans

Prepaid plans require you to pay before using services. You buy a SIM card, top up your balance, and subscribe to a bundle. Prepaid is flexible because you can change plans every month and avoid long-term contracts. It is the best option for visitors, short-term residents, and anyone who wants strict control over spending.

Postpaid plans bill you at the end of each month based on your usage. They usually include larger data allowances, unlimited local calls, and sometimes device installment plans. Postpaid is convenient for heavy users and families who want predictable monthly costs. However, cancelling a postpaid contract early may involve fees.

Most operators allow you to switch from prepaid to postpaid without changing your number. If you are unsure which type to choose, start with prepaid. You can always upgrade once you understand your usage patterns.

Data Allowances and 5G

Data is the main factor for most users. Plans range from a few gigabytes per month to unlimited data with fair-use policies. Unlimited plans usually slow down after a certain threshold, such as 100 GB or 200 GB. Check the fair-use limit before signing up, especially if you stream video or work from home.

5G coverage is available in major cities including Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam, and Khobar. STC generally leads in 5G availability, followed by Mobily and Zain. Virgin Mobile uses STC’s network, so it offers the same coverage as STC. If you live in a rural area, check coverage maps before choosing an operator.

Many plans include social media data that does not count against your main allowance. This means apps like WhatsApp, Twitter, Snapchat, and YouTube may be free or partially free. Verify which apps are included, as this can significantly affect your data consumption.

International Calls and Roaming

If you call family abroad, compare international calling bundles. Operators offer add-ons for specific countries or regions such as South Asia, the Philippines, Egypt, or India. Without a bundle, international calls can be expensive. Some apps like WhatsApp and FaceTime allow voice and video calls over data, which can reduce costs.

For travel outside Saudi Arabia, check roaming packages before departure. Most operators offer daily or weekly roaming bundles for popular destinations. Using your phone without a roaming package can lead to very high charges. You can also buy a local SIM at your destination if your phone is unlocked.

Some plans include free incoming SMS abroad, which is useful for receiving banking or government verification codes. Ask your operator about this feature if you travel frequently.

How to Buy a SIM Card

To buy a SIM card in Saudi Arabia, you need a valid ID. Citizens use their national ID, while residents need their iqama. Visitors on tourist or visit visas can buy prepaid SIM cards at airports, malls, and operator shops by showing their passport and visa. The seller registers the SIM to your identity, which is required by law.

You can buy a SIM at official operator stores, supermarkets, electronics shops, or online through operator websites and apps. Airport kiosks are convenient but may have limited plan choices. Shopping around can save money and give you more data.

After inserting the SIM, follow the activation instructions. You may need to make a first call or use the operator’s app to activate the line. Keep the SIM packaging and receipt until the line is fully active.

Managing Your Plan and Avoiding Extra Charges

Download your operator’s mobile app to monitor data usage, renew bundles, and pay bills. Most apps are available in Arabic and English. Setting usage alerts helps you avoid running out of data unexpectedly. If you do run out, you can buy extra data passes.

Be careful with auto-renewal. Many prepaid bundles renew automatically if you have enough balance. This is convenient but can drain your credit if you forget to cancel. Check your balance and active subscriptions regularly.

Postpaid customers should review their monthly bills for unexpected charges. If you see a charge you do not recognize, contact customer service. The Communications, Space and Technology Commission provides a complaint channel if the operator does not resolve your issue.

Switching Operators and Number Portability

If you are unhappy with your operator, you can switch while keeping your number. Number portability is available for both prepaid and postpaid customers. Start by requesting a porting authorization code from your current operator. Then visit the new operator and provide the code along with your ID or iqama.

Before switching, settle any outstanding bills and cancel auto-renewal subscriptions. Porting usually takes a few hours to one business day. During the transfer, you may experience a short service interruption. Keep your old SIM in the phone until the new one activates.

Compare offers carefully before switching. A cheaper plan may have weaker coverage or slower data speeds. Read reviews from people in your neighborhood and test the network with a prepaid SIM before committing to a long-term postpaid contract.

eSIM vs Physical SIM Cards

Modern smartphones support eSIM, a digital SIM built into the phone. In Saudi Arabia, STC, Mobily, and Zain offer eSIM activation for compatible devices. eSIM is convenient because you can activate a plan without visiting a store. It also lets you use two phone numbers on one device.

Not all phones support eSIM, and not all plans are available on eSIM. If your device is older or imported, you may need a physical SIM. Activation usually requires scanning a QR code provided by the operator or using the operator’s app.

eSIM is useful for travelers and people who switch plans frequently. If you lose your phone, the eSIM profile can be deactivated remotely through the operator. This adds a layer of security compared to a physical card that could be removed and used.

Customer Service and Support Channels

Each operator offers customer service through phone hotlines, mobile apps, social media, and physical stores. STC and Mobily have the largest store networks, while Virgin Mobile operates mainly through its app and chat support. Before choosing an operator, consider how easy it is to get help when you need it.

Many common issues can be resolved through the app, such as checking balance, activating bundles, and paying bills. For more complex problems like billing disputes or network outages, phone support or a store visit may be needed. Keep your account number or iqama ready when contacting support.

If the operator does not resolve your issue, you can escalate to the Communications, Space and Technology Commission. The commission has a consumer complaints system and can mediate disputes. Keeping records of your complaint helps the process.

Number Portability Step by Step

Switching operators while keeping your number is straightforward. First, make sure your account is active and not locked. Request a porting authorization code from your current operator, usually by sending an SMS or using the app. The code is valid for a limited time.

Visit the new operator with your ID or iqama and the porting code. Choose a plan and complete the registration. The new operator submits the porting request to the regulatory system. Once approved, your number moves to the new network.

During the transfer, you may lose service for a few hours. Do not cancel your old plan until the new line is working. Test calls, texts, and data after activation. If the transfer fails, both operators can explain the reason.

Data Rollover and Promotional Offers

Some operators allow unused data to roll over to the next month, while others reset the allowance. Data rollover is valuable if your usage varies. Check the rollover rules, including limits and expiry dates, before choosing a plan.

Promotional offers are common during holidays and shopping festivals. These may include double data, free social media usage, or discounted international minutes. While attractive, read the fine print. Some offers require auto-renewal or have usage restrictions.

Loyalty programs reward long-term customers with points, discounts, or partner offers. Ask about these programs when signing up. Used wisely, they can add significant value over time.

Choosing Family and Heavy-Use Plans

Family plans allow multiple lines to share a pool of data and minutes. They are usually cheaper than separate individual plans. The main account holder manages payments and can add or remove members. Family plans are ideal for households with several smartphone users.

Heavy users who stream video, work remotely, or use mobile hotspot should look for plans with high data allowances or unlimited data. Be aware of fair-use policies that reduce speeds after a threshold. Unlimited plans may also prioritize certain apps or services.

Compare the total cost of a family plan against individual plans. Sometimes two or three separate plans are cheaper. Use your operator’s app to estimate usage and find the best fit.

Buying a Phone in Saudi Arabia

Mobile phones are widely available in Saudi Arabia at operator stores, electronics shops, and online retailers. Prices are competitive, especially during promotional periods. Make sure the phone supports the network bands used by Saudi operators, particularly for 5G.

Phones purchased from operators are often locked to that network for a period if bought with an installment plan. If you want flexibility, buy an unlocked phone outright. Check the warranty terms and whether international brands have authorized service centers in the Kingdom.

When buying a used phone, verify that it is not reported stolen and that it works with your chosen operator. Meet in a safe public place and test all functions before paying. Saving the original receipt helps with warranty claims.

Mobile Plans for Visitors and Short-Term Residents

Visitors who stay for a few weeks can buy prepaid SIM cards with tourist bundles. These bundles include data, local minutes, and sometimes international minutes. They are sold at airports and operator stores with simple activation.

Short-term residents on work visit visas can also use prepaid plans until their iqama is issued. Once the iqama is ready, they can switch to regular resident plans or postpaid contracts. Keeping the same number is possible if the account is registered properly.

For very short trips, some travelers prefer international roaming packages from their home country. Compare costs carefully, as local Saudi SIM cards are often cheaper for data and local calls. Having a local number also makes it easier to use ride-hailing and delivery apps.

Testing Network Speed and Coverage

Before committing to a long-term plan, test the network in the places you use most. Most operators offer prepaid SIMs that let you check data speeds, call quality, and indoor coverage. Download a speed test app and measure performance at home, work, and on your commute.

Network performance can vary by neighborhood and building. A tower that is fast outdoors may provide weak signals inside concrete buildings. Ask neighbors or colleagues about their experience with different operators in your area.

If you find that your chosen operator is slow after signing a contract, contact customer service. Sometimes changing a setting or replacing the SIM improves performance. In persistent cases, number portability lets you switch without losing your number.

Recycling Old Phones and SIM Cards

When upgrading your phone, do not throw the old device in the trash. Saudi Arabia has electronic waste recycling programs and some retailers accept old phones for trade-in. Deleting personal data and removing the SIM card protects your privacy.

Old SIM cards should be cut or destroyed before disposal, especially if they were linked to banking or government services. Some operators allow you to return unused SIM cards at their stores. Recycling reduces environmental impact and keeps materials out of landfills.

If you sell your old phone, perform a factory reset and remove all accounts. Buyers may try to recover data from improperly reset devices. Taking a few minutes to clean the device protects your identity.

Telecom Consumer Rights in Saudi Arabia

The Communications, Space and Technology Commission protects telecom consumers. Operators must provide clear contracts, accurate billing, and a way to complain. They must also explain fair-use policies and any speed restrictions before you sign up.

If an operator charges you for services you did not request, you have the right to dispute the charge. Request a detailed bill and escalate to the commission if the operator does not resolve the issue. Keep records of all communications.

Number portability, contract cancellation rules, and compensation for service outages are regulated. Knowing your rights helps you push back against unfair practices and choose operators that respect their customers.

Frequently asked questions

Which mobile operator has the best coverage in Saudi Arabia?

STC generally has the widest coverage, especially in rural areas, but Mobily and Zain are strong in cities.

Can I buy a SIM card as a visitor?

Yes. Visitors can buy prepaid SIM cards by showing a passport and visa at airports, malls, or operator shops.

What is the difference between prepaid and postpaid?

Prepaid requires payment in advance with no contract. Postpaid bills you monthly and often includes more data and minutes.

Is 5G available in Saudi Arabia?

Yes. 5G is available in major cities and expanding across the country.

How do I avoid high international call charges?

Use international calling bundles, WhatsApp calls over data, or buy a local SIM when traveling.

Can I keep my number if I switch operators?

Yes. Number portability is available. Request a porting code from your current operator and give it to the new one.

What documents do I need to buy a SIM?

Citizens need a national ID, residents need an iqama, and visitors need a passport and visa.
