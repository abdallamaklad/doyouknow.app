Saudi Driving License: Step-by-Step Guide

doyouknow.app Editorial Team2026-07-094 min read

Editorial note: This guide was checked against Saudi Traffic, Ministry of Interior, Absher, driving school, and Ministry of Health sources on 2026-07-09. Fees, exempt countries, and school rules can change, so confirm details through official channels before applying.

Driving in Saudi Arabia is often a practical necessity. Cities are spread out, summer heat is intense, and many daily routines are easier with a car. This guide explains how citizens and residents can get or renew a Saudi driving license, based on the Arabic practical guide.

Who can apply?

  • Saudi citizens who meet the age and health requirements.
  • Residents with a valid iqama.
  • Applicants who pass the medical check and required training or tests.
  • Some holders of foreign licenses may be eligible for conversion or exemption depending on the country and current Saudi Traffic rules.

Required documents

  • Valid iqama or national ID.
  • Valid passport for residents where requested.
  • Recent personal photos if the school or center requires them.
  • Medical check result from an approved clinic or connected medical center.
  • Existing foreign license if applying for exchange or exemption.

Step 1: Check eligibility

Before paying for a school package, confirm whether you are applying as a beginner, an experienced driver, or a foreign-license holder who may qualify for a shorter process. Rules depend on your license country, residency status, and current Saudi Traffic procedures.

Step 2: Register with a driving school

If you need training, register with an approved Saudi driving school. The Arabic source lists typical requirements as a valid iqama, passport, personal photos, medical check, and registration fees. Fees vary by school and city; the Arabic guide gives a broad range of SAR 2,000-4,500 for school registration depending on the program.

Step 3: Complete the medical check

The medical check is usually simple: eyesight, blood pressure, and general fitness. It can be done through an approved clinic, sometimes inside or linked to the driving school. The Arabic guide lists a typical cost of about SAR 100-200, with the result sent electronically.

Step 4: Theory training

The theoretical course covers Saudi traffic laws, road signs, safety rules, driving in difficult weather, and what to do after accidents. Many schools provide the theory course online. The Arabic guide gives a typical duration of 4 to 8 hours before the theory test.

Step 5: Practical training

Practical lessons cover starting, stopping, turning, U-turns, parking, highway driving, night driving, and safe road behavior. Experienced drivers may need fewer lessons; beginners may need more. The Arabic source lists roughly 10-15 lessons for experienced drivers and 20-30 lessons for beginners, with each lesson lasting about 45-60 minutes.

Step 6: Take the driving test

The test can include a closed-course field test and a road test. You may be asked to reverse, park, turn, start on a slope, follow signs, handle signals, and drive confidently in traffic. If you fail, you can retake the test after the waiting period set by the school.

Step 7: Receive the license

After passing, the license is issued electronically through Absher, and a physical copy can be requested through Saudi Traffic channels. The Arabic guide states that licenses are generally valid for 10 years for Saudi citizens and 5 years for residents, with renewal handled electronically.

Driving licenses for women

Since 2018, women have had the legal right to drive in Saudi Arabia. Saudi women and resident women follow the same licensing framework, with women's driving schools and female instructors available in many cities.

Estimated total cost

  • Registration and training: SAR 2,000-4,500 depending on school and track.
  • Medical check: about SAR 100-200.
  • Extra lessons: about SAR 100-150 per lesson.
  • Testing and issue fees: roughly SAR 200-400.
  • Total for a beginner: roughly SAR 3,500-7,000.
  • Total for an experienced driver: roughly SAR 2,500-4,000.

Mistakes that can cause failure

  • Not checking mirrors before turns or lane changes.
  • Not stopping fully at the stop line.
  • Driving too fast or too slowly for conditions.
  • Panicking during U-turns or parking maneuvers.
  • Forgetting the seat belt before starting.

Renewing a Saudi driving license

Renewal is normally handled through Absher after paying the fee and completing the required medical check. Residents should also confirm that their iqama is valid before renewing.

Frequently asked questions

Can a resident get a Saudi driving license without a test?

Possibly, if the resident has a license from an exempt country. Confirm the current list through Saudi Traffic or Absher.

How long does the process take?

The Arabic source gives a broad range of two weeks to two months, depending on lesson availability and test results.

Can women drive in Saudi Arabia?

Yes. Since 2018, women can legally drive and apply for licenses in Saudi Arabia.
