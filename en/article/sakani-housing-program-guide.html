Sakani Housing Program: Eligibility, Products, and How to Apply

What Is Sakani?

If you live in Saudi Arabia, you have almost certainly heard someone ask "did you check your Sakani status?" — around the office, in family gatherings, or across social media every time new housing support batches are announced. Sakani, from the Arabic word for dwelling, is the Kingdom's unified national housing platform: the single place where Saudi citizens check their eligibility for housing support, choose a product, and follow the journey to owning a home.

Launched in 2017 under the Vision 2030 Housing Program, Sakani is jointly operated by the Ministry of Municipalities and Housing (MOMAH) and the Real Estate Development Fund (REDF). The goal is ambitious: raise Saudi home ownership toward 70 percent by 2030. By the mid-2020s, official figures put the national ownership rate around 65 percent — up from below half when the vision launched — with Sakani serving well over a hundred thousand families per year. This guide explains what the program offers, who qualifies, and how the application actually works.

Who Runs Sakani and Why It Exists

Before Sakani, getting housing support in Saudi Arabia meant navigating separate queues at the Real Estate Development Fund, the Ministry of Housing, and individual banks, with waiting lists that could stretch for years. The 2017 redesign flipped the model: the citizen-facing platform handles eligibility and product selection in one journey, while the institutions work in the background. The ministry sets policy and eligibility rules; REDF finances the subsidies; banks and finance companies issue the mortgages; developers supply the units.

The platform reaches citizens through four channels: the sakani.sa website, the mobile app, physical Sakani centers in major cities such as Riyadh, Jeddah, Madinah, and Alkhobar, and a unified call center. Behind it sits the Vision 2030 Housing Program, which treats home ownership as an economic stabilizer — supporting construction jobs, banking growth, and household wealth at the same time.

Delivery happens continuously, not once a year. The ministry announces handovers month by month — hundreds of units at a time delivered to beneficiary families in projects across the Kingdom — and publishes the numbers through its newsroom and the Saudi Press Agency. That steady drumbeat of announcements is usually what sends "Sakani" trending again.

Eligibility: Who Can Apply?

The core conditions have stayed broadly stable. An applicant must be a Saudi national, apply as the head of a family — with singles generally eligible from age 25 — be resident in the Kingdom, and not have owned a suitable home during the five years before applying. A household can receive housing support only once, so the system checks that neither the applicant nor family members have benefited previously.

The regulations also define priority and special cases. Widows and divorced women with dependent children can apply in their own right after defined waiting periods. Orphans, people with disabilities, and families registered in social security have dedicated tracks, including the "developmental housing" route run with the Ministry of Human Resources and Social Development for households least able to afford market prices.

Checking is instant rather than a paperwork queue. You log in to the platform through Nafath, the national single sign-on linked to your verified digital identity, answer a short household questionnaire, and the system cross-references government records before returning an eligibility result — often within minutes.

Sakani Products and Subsidies

The flagship product is the subsidized mortgage (al-qard al-mad'um). A participating bank or finance company lends you the money, and REDF covers some or all of the financing profit on up to SAR 500,000 of the principal — 100 percent of the profit for the lowest-income beneficiaries, with the subsidy share tapering as income rises. A down-payment support grant, worth up to SAR 150,000 for qualifying low-income first-time buyers, has also been offered in recent years. Because subsidy percentages and grant rules are revised periodically, the figures shown in the app at the moment you apply are the ones that count.

On the housing side, beneficiaries choose between several paths. Ready units — villas, apartments, or duplexes from the ministry and partner developers — suit families who want to move in immediately. Off-plan units, sold under the Wafi off-plan sales program with payments tied to construction milestones, usually cost less per square meter. Designated residential land plots remain an option in some regions, and the self-construction product supports beneficiaries who already own land, releasing financing in stages as building progresses.

There is also a route for households that cannot carry a mortgage at all. Developmental housing matches social-security families with subsidized or donated units through partnerships with non-profit organizations and the private sector — a reminder that the program aims at the full income spectrum, not just salaried applicants.

How to Apply, Step by Step

The journey starts in the Sakani app or on sakani.sa. Log in with Nafath, complete the household questionnaire, and receive your eligibility decision. If you are eligible, the platform immediately shows which products you can take. You will need your national ID and family register details; later stages require a Saudi bank account in your name for disbursements — our guide to opening a bank account in Saudi Arabia covers that process if you need it.

Next comes financing. The built-in mortgage advisor estimates how much you can borrow and what subsidy applies, then lets you compare offers from participating banks and finance companies inside the platform. After you pick a product — a ready unit, an off-plan home, land, or self-build — you sign the financing contract electronically, and the allocation is registered with the ministry and REDF.

Keep your data current after applying. The ministry asks beneficiaries to update contact and income information, warns against unofficial links that claim to check eligibility, and handles questions through its unified call center and Sakani branches. Announcements about new projects, delivery batches, and updated terms appear first on the ministry's website and the Saudi Press Agency, so those are the sources worth following — our explainer on property ownership rules in Saudi Arabia adds wider context on the market Sakani operates in.

FAQ

What is the Sakani program?

Sakani is Saudi Arabia's national housing platform, launched in 2017 under the Vision 2030 Housing Program and run by the Ministry of Municipalities and Housing with the Real Estate Development Fund. It lets Saudi citizens check eligibility for housing support and access subsidized mortgages, housing units, land, and self-build financing in one place.

Who is eligible for Sakani?

Broadly, Saudi nationals applying as heads of family — singles generally from age 25 — who live in the Kingdom, have not owned a suitable home in the past five years, and have not received housing support before. Widows, divorced women with children, orphans, and social-security families have dedicated or priority tracks.

What is a subsidized mortgage under Sakani?

It is a mortgage from a participating bank where the Real Estate Development Fund pays part or all of the financing profit on up to SAR 500,000 of the principal. The lowest-income beneficiaries receive a full profit subsidy, with the share decreasing as income rises.

How do I check my Sakani eligibility?

Log in to the Sakani app or sakani.sa using Nafath national single sign-on and complete the household questionnaire. Most applicants receive an instant result without submitting paper documents.

Does Sakani offer free land?

Sakani has offered designated residential land plots as one of its products in some regions, subject to availability and allocation batches. The self-construction option also supports beneficiaries who already own land and want to build on it with staged financing.
