UAE Visa System Overview

The United Arab Emirates welcomed over 17 million international visitors in 2024, and Dubai remains the top entry point for most travelers. Whether you are planning a short holiday, a business trip, or considering long-term residency, understanding the Dubai tourist visa landscape is essential.

The UAE offers a tiered visa system designed for different travel purposes and durations. From a quick 48-hour transit stop to a 10-year Golden Visa, the options can feel overwhelming. This guide breaks down every UAE visa type, including fees, processing times, eligibility, and step-by-step application instructions.

Before applying, every traveler should know three key facts: most visas are now issued electronically through the ICA or GDRFAD portals, visa-free entry is available for citizens of over 80 countries, and overstaying any visa incurs fines of AED 50 per day.

All 11 UAE Visa Types Explained

The UAE government currently offers 11 main visa categories for tourists, visitors, and prospective residents. The right choice depends on your nationality, travel purpose, planned duration of stay, and long-term intentions.

Single-Entry Tourist Visa (30 & 60 Days)

The standard Dubai tourist visa is the most common choice for leisure travelers. It comes in two durations: 30 days and 60 days from the date of entry. Both are single-entry visas, meaning if you leave the UAE, you cannot re-enter on the same visa.

Who needs it: Citizens of countries that do not qualify for visa-free entry or visa on arrival. This includes most South Asian, African, and some Southeast Asian nationalities.

Key requirements:

  • Passport valid for at least 6 months beyond the date of entry
  • Confirmed return flight ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or host invitation)
  • Bank statements showing sufficient funds (typically AED 3,000–5,000 equivalent)
  • Travel insurance covering the UAE (mandatory)

Application: Apply through the Federal Authority for Identity and Citizenship (ICA) portal, a UAE-based airline (Emirates, Etihad, FlyDubai), or an authorized hotel sponsor. Processing typically takes 3–5 working days.

Fees: The 30-day visa costs approximately AED 350–400, while the 60-day visa ranges from AED 650–750 depending on the channel and urgency.

Multiple-Entry Tourist Visa

For travelers who need to enter and exit the UAE multiple times within a single trip window, the multiple-entry tourist visa is the ideal solution. It is available for both 30-day and 60-day validity periods.

This visa suits business travelers attending meetings across GCC countries, tourists combining Dubai with Oman or Bahrain trips, and families visiting relatives who also plan side trips. Unlike the single-entry variant, you may leave and re-enter the UAE as many times as needed within the visa validity period.

Fees: Expect to pay AED 750–900 for a 30-day multiple-entry visa and AED 1,200–1,500 for the 60-day option. Processing time is 3–7 working days.

5-Year Multiple-Entry Tourist Visa

Introduced in 2021, the 5-year multiple-entry tourist visa allows holders to stay up to 90 days per visit, with a total maximum of 180 days per year. This is a game-changer for frequent visitors, property investors, and business consultants who regularly travel to Dubai.

Requirements:

  • Proof of health insurance valid in the UAE
  • Bank statements showing a balance of at least AED 15,000 (or equivalent) over the past 6 months
  • Valid passport with at least 6 months remaining
  • Proof of onward travel or return ticket

Fee: Approximately AED 2,500–3,000. Processing can take 7–15 working days due to additional financial verification.

Transit Visa (48 & 96 Hours)

Dubai International Airport is one of the world's busiest transit hubs. If your layover is longer than 8 hours and you want to explore the city, a transit visa is your best option.

The 48-hour transit visa is free and issued on arrival for eligible travelers. The 96-hour (4-day) transit visa must be applied for in advance through a UAE airline or travel agent and costs approximately AED 150–200.

Important conditions: You must have a confirmed onward ticket to a third destination (not your country of origin), and the layover must meet minimum duration requirements. Transit visas cannot be extended or converted to tourist visas.

Golden Visa (10-Year Residency)

The UAE Golden Visa is a long-term residency program that grants holders a 10-year renewable visa without requiring a local sponsor. It is one of the most attractive residency schemes in the Middle East.

Eligible categories include:

  • Investors in real estate (property value AED 2 million or more)
  • Entrepreneurs with approved business ventures
  • Exceptional talents in culture, art, sports, and digital technology
  • Scientists and researchers with significant achievements
  • Outstanding students and graduates from top universities
  • Skilled professionals in medicine, engineering, IT, and science (minimum bachelor's degree, salary AED 30,000+/month)

Benefits: No local sponsor needed, ability to sponsor family members and domestic workers, 100% foreign business ownership in mainland UAE, and visa stays valid if the holder stays outside the UAE for over 6 months.

Fee: AED 3,000–5,000 depending on the category and processing channel.

Green Visa (5-Year Residency)

The Green Visa targets skilled professionals, freelancers, and self-employed individuals who want a longer-term base in the UAE without the full Golden Visa requirements.

Freelancer/self-employed requirements: Bachelor's degree or specialized diploma, freelance permit from a UAE authority, and annual income of at least AED 360,000 from self-employment over the past 2 years (or financial solvency).

Skilled employee requirements: Bachelor's degree, employment contract, minimum salary of AED 15,000/month, and classification in occupational levels 1, 2, or 3 under the Ministry of Human Resources.

Fee: AED 2,500–3,500. Processing time: 5–10 working days.

Job Exploration Visa

Launched to attract global talent, the Job Exploration Visa allows skilled professionals to enter the UAE for a specified period to search for employment without needing a sponsor.

Requirements: Bachelor's degree (or equivalent), classified in skill levels 1, 2, or 3, and financial solvency proof. The visa is issued for 60, 90, or 120 days depending on the fee tier.

Fees: AED 1,500–2,500 depending on duration. This visa cannot be renewed, but if you secure a job, your employer can transition you to an employment residence visa.

Student Visa

The UAE student visa is sponsored by the accredited educational institution where the student is enrolled. It is typically issued for one year and renewable annually based on continued enrollment.

Many universities now offer long-term student visas for outstanding students, and some elite institutions can sponsor visas for up to 5 years for specific programs like medicine and engineering.

Requirements: Acceptance letter from a UAE-accredited institution, valid passport, medical fitness test, and health insurance. Part-time work is permitted in some emirates under strict guidelines.

Retirement Visa

The UAE offers a 5-year renewable retirement visa for individuals aged 55 and above who meet certain financial criteria. This has made Dubai and Abu Dhabi increasingly popular retirement destinations.

Requirements (meet one of the following):

  • AED 1 million in property investment
  • AED 1 million in savings (cash or fixed deposits)
  • Monthly income of at least AED 20,000

Fee: Approximately AED 2,000–3,000. Processing time: 7–14 working days.

Family Reunion Visa

Residence visa holders can sponsor family members through the family reunion visa program. The sponsor's minimum salary requirement varies by emirate but generally starts at AED 4,000/month with accommodation provided by the employer.

Female residents can sponsor their husbands and children under certain conditions, including holding a residence permit in specialized categories and meeting higher salary thresholds (typically AED 10,000+/month).

Key note: All sponsored family members over 18 must pass a medical fitness test at an approved health center in the UAE.

How to Apply Step-by-Step

Applying for a Dubai tourist visa is straightforward if you follow the correct sequence. Here is the standard process for most tourist visa categories:

Step 1: Determine Your Visa Type

Check your nationality against the UAE's visa-free list on the ICP website. If you require a visa, decide between single-entry, multiple-entry, or long-term options based on your travel plan.

Step 2: Gather Required Documents

Standard documents include: passport copy (valid 6+ months), passport-sized photo with white background, confirmed flight booking, hotel reservation or host Emirates ID, bank statements, and travel insurance.

Step 3: Submit Application

Apply through the ICA Smart Services portal, a UAE airline (Emirates, Etihad, Air Arabia, FlyDubai), or an authorized hotel/travel agency. Each channel has slightly different forms and fees.

Step 4: Pay Fees and Wait

Payment is accepted via credit card on the ICA portal. Standard processing is 3–5 days. Express processing (24–48 hours) is available for an additional AED 300–500.

Step 5: Receive e-Visa

Approved visas are sent by email as a PDF. Print a copy and save a digital backup on your phone. You do not need a visa stamp in your passport before travel.

Step 6: Entry and Immigration

At Dubai Airport, proceed to immigration with your passport and e-visa printout. The officer will verify your documents and stamp your entry date. Your visa validity starts from this entry date, not the issue date.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Thousands of UAE visa applications are delayed or rejected every year due to preventable errors. Here are the most common mistakes:

  • Overstaying: Even a one-day overstay triggers fines of AED 50/day and a potential entry ban. Set reminders before your visa expires.
  • Passport validity: Many travelers arrive with passports valid for less than 6 months. Airlines often deny boarding.
  • Incorrect photo specs: UAE visa photos require a white background, specific dimensions (35mm x 45mm), and no head coverings except for religious purposes with documentation.
  • Applying too early: Tourist visas are typically valid for entry within 60 days of issuance. Apply 2–4 weeks before travel.
  • Wrong sponsor type: Some nationalities must apply through specific channels (airline, hotel, or direct ICA). Using the wrong channel leads to rejection.
  • Insufficient funds: Immigration officers may ask for proof of funds at entry. Carry recent bank statements or a credit card with adequate limit.
  • Missing travel insurance: Travel insurance is now mandatory for most tourist visas. Policies must cover COVID-19 and emergency medical treatment.
  • Job hunting on a tourist visa: Working on a tourist visa is illegal and can result in deportation and a ban. Use the Job Exploration Visa instead.

FAQ

Which nationalities get visa-free entry to Dubai?

Citizens of over 80 countries receive visa-free entry to the UAE. This includes all GCC nationals (Saudi Arabia, Oman, Qatar, Bahrain, Kuwait), most European countries, the USA, Canada, Australia, Japan, South Korea, and several others. Visa-free stays range from 30 to 90 days depending on nationality. Check the ICP website for the latest list.

Can I extend my Dubai tourist visa?

Yes, 30-day and 60-day tourist visas can be extended once for an additional 30 days without leaving the country. The extension fee is approximately AED 800–1,000 and must be applied for before the original visa expires. The 5-year multiple-entry visa cannot be extended per visit beyond 90 days.

How long does Dubai visa processing take?

Standard tourist visa processing takes 3–5 working days. Express processing is available in 24–48 hours for an additional fee. Golden Visa and retirement visa applications take 2–4 weeks due to extra document verification.

Why was my Dubai visa rejected?

Common reasons include: passport validity under 6 months, previous overstay or violation, criminal record, security concerns, applying through an unauthorized agent, missing documents, or photo non-compliance. The UAE does not always provide detailed rejection reasons.

Are Dubai visa fees refundable if rejected?

No. UAE visa fees are non-refundable regardless of the application outcome. Always double-check your documents before applying to avoid losing the fee.

Can I get a Dubai visa on arrival?

Citizens of selected countries including the USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, and most EU countries can obtain a visa on arrival at Dubai Airport. This is typically valid for 30 or 90 days depending on nationality. Indian citizens holding a valid US visa, EU residence permit, or UK residence visa can also get a 14-day visa on arrival.

Do children need a separate visa?

Yes, every traveler including infants requires an individual visa or must be included on a parent's visa. Some airlines offer discounted visa fees for children under 18 when applied together with a parent.

Is travel insurance mandatory for a Dubai visa?

Yes, travel insurance covering medical emergencies and COVID-19 is mandatory for most tourist visa categories. Some airlines bundle insurance into the visa application fee. Ensure your policy is valid for the entire duration of your stay.

What is the difference between Golden Visa and Green Visa?

The Golden Visa is a 10-year residency visa for investors, exceptional talents, and highly skilled professionals. The Green Visa is a 5-year residency for skilled employees, freelancers, and self-employed individuals. Both allow self-sponsorship and family sponsorship, but the Golden Visa has higher eligibility thresholds and longer validity.

Can I work in Dubai on a tourist visa?

No. Working on a tourist visa is illegal and punishable by fines, deportation, and a potential entry ban. If you want to work in Dubai, your employer must sponsor an employment residence visa. Use the Job Exploration Visa if you want to search for employment before securing a contract.
