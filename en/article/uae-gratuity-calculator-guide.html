What gratuity is and what it is not

End-of-service gratuity is a statutory payment connected to employment tenure. It is not a bonus, commission, pension, or replacement for unpaid salary. The calculation usually starts with the employee’s basic salary and completed service period, then checks whether the employee qualifies and whether any lawful deductions or unpaid amounts affect the final settlement.

Why basic salary matters

Many disputes begin because employees calculate gratuity from total salary while employers calculate from basic salary. Housing, transport, schooling, sales incentives, and other allowances may be paid every month, but they are not always part of the gratuity base. Before estimating anything, find the basic salary written in the contract or payroll system.

Service period and eligibility

The completed period of continuous service is central. Employees should identify the joining date, last working day, unpaid leave periods, and any breaks in employment. A small difference in dates can change the estimate, especially around the first year or around a completed additional year. Keep joining documents and resignation or termination notices.

The common calculation logic

For many private-sector employees, the familiar logic is 21 days of basic salary for each of the first five years of service, then 30 days of basic salary for each additional year, subject to legal conditions and caps. This article uses that as an orientation framework only. Always confirm the current rule, contract type, and applicable authority before relying on a number.

Resignation, termination, and disputes

A resignation file and a termination file can feel different even when the calculation uses similar documents. Notice, unused leave, unpaid wages, deductions, and company property may affect the final settlement conversation. Ask for a written breakdown that separates gratuity from salary arrears, leave encashment, notice pay, and deductions.

If the output differs from the employer’s calculation, compare assumptions before alleging bad faith. The disagreement may be a date, unpaid leave period, salary component, or cap.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is gratuity calculated on gross salary?

Usually the starting point is basic salary, not total monthly pay. Check the current rule and your contract before relying on an estimate.

Do allowances count in UAE gratuity?

Allowances are not always part of the gratuity base. Separate basic salary from allowances before calculating.

Can a calculator give the final legal answer?

No. It gives an estimate. Final settlement depends on dates, salary records, deductions, leave, and the applicable rule.

Should I sign a final settlement immediately?

Read the itemized calculation first. If figures are unclear or disputed, ask for clarification before acknowledging final receipt.

Is this legal advice?

No. It is an editorial guide. Confirm current rules with MOHRE, UAE legislation sources, or a qualified adviser.
