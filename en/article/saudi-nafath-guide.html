Nafath Saudi Arabia: Register, Log In, Access Services

doyouknow.app Editorial Team2026-07-1014 min read

Editorial note: This guide was checked against official Saudi government sources and service portals on 2026-07-10. Fees, procedures, and eligibility rules can change, so confirm details through the official channels linked below before acting.

What Is Nafath?

Nafath is Saudi Arabia’s national unified access platform that lets citizens and residents log into many government services with a single digital identity. Instead of creating a separate username and password for every ministry or platform, users can authenticate through Nafath and access approved services securely. It is part of Saudi Arabia’s broader digital transformation goals under Vision 2030.

Nafath works alongside Absher, but serves a different purpose. Absher is a service portal that handles many transactions directly, while Nafath is an identity layer that other government systems use to verify who you are. When you visit a participating government website or app, you may see a “Login with Nafath” button. Selecting it sends an authentication request to your Nafath mobile app.

The platform reduces the number of passwords users must remember, lowers the risk of weak passwords, and gives the government a standardized way to manage digital identities. For residents, understanding Nafath makes it easier to use new online services as they become available.

Why Nafath Matters for Residents

As more Saudi government services move online, secure and convenient login becomes essential. Nafath provides a central identity that links your national ID or iqama to your mobile phone and biometric data. Once registered, you can approve login requests with a tap on your phone, often using fingerprint or face recognition.

Nafath is especially useful for people who use multiple platforms. A resident might need to log into Absher for iqama services, the Ministry of Interior for traffic fines, the Ministry of Human Resources for work permits, and the Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority for tax matters. With Nafath, the same trusted identity works across these systems without storing the same password on each site.

The platform also improves security. Each login request appears on your phone with details about the requesting service. You can approve or reject the request. This makes it much harder for attackers to access your account even if they know part of your password, because they would also need your unlocked phone.

How to Register for Nafath

Registration for Nafath is done through the Nafath mobile app, available on the Apple App Store and Google Play Store. After installing the app, you enter your national ID number for citizens or your iqama number for residents. The system checks your identity against official records. You then verify your mobile number with a one-time code.

The next step is setting up authentication factors. Nafath typically requires a device passcode and supports biometric unlock such as fingerprint or Face ID. You may also set a Nafath-specific PIN. During setup, the app displays a list of terms and conditions. Reading these carefully helps you understand how your data is used and what to do if your phone is lost.

After registration, the app may prompt you to link other services or confirm your email address. Some users need to visit an Absher activation kiosk or a service center if the system cannot verify their identity automatically. This is common for new residents or people whose records need updating.

How to Log In Using Nafath

When you visit a government website or app that supports Nafath, look for the Nafath login option. After selecting it, the page asks for your national ID or iqama number. Enter the number and confirm. The system then sends a push notification to your Nafath app.

Open the notification or launch the Nafath app. You will see a login request showing the name of the service, the time, and your location. Review the details carefully. If everything looks correct, approve the request using your biometric method or PIN. The website or app then logs you in automatically.

If you do not receive the notification, check that your phone is connected to the internet and that notifications for the Nafath app are enabled. You can also generate a one-time code inside the app and enter it manually on the website. This backup option is useful when push notifications are delayed.

Linking and Managing Government Services

After registering with Nafath, many services become accessible without separate registration. However, some platforms may ask you to complete a one-time linking step the first time you log in. This step usually involves confirming your contact details and accepting the service’s terms of use.

You can view linked services inside the Nafath app. This dashboard helps you understand which government systems have access to your identity information. If you no longer use a service, you can unlink it. Regularly reviewing linked services is a good security habit and helps prevent unused accounts from remaining active.

Nafath also supports delegated access in some cases. For example, a company representative may use Nafath to authenticate on behalf of a business for certain government portals. The rules for delegation depend on the service and the user’s role. Always follow official instructions when setting up delegated access.

Common Nafath Issues and Fixes

A frequent issue is not receiving the push notification. This can happen due to poor internet, disabled notifications, or a delayed server response. Make sure the app is updated, your phone has a stable connection, and notifications are allowed. If the problem continues, use the one-time code option inside the app.

Another common problem is identity verification failure during registration. This may occur if your mobile number is not registered in your name, your iqama records are outdated, or your biometric data does not match. Updating your mobile number through your telecom provider and verifying your Absher profile often resolves this. In some cases, a visit to a service center is necessary.

Users who change phones must reinstall the Nafath app and register the new device. You may need to verify your identity again. If your old phone is lost or stolen, contact Nafath support or visit a service center to block the device and prevent unauthorized access. Never share your Nafath PIN or biometric unlock with anyone.

Security Best Practices

Protect your Nafath account as carefully as you protect your passport. Use a strong device passcode and enable biometric authentication. Do not approve login requests from services you did not initiate. If you receive an unexpected Nafath request, reject it and change your Absher or related passwords.

Keep the Nafath app updated. Updates often include security improvements and support for new services. Avoid using public Wi-Fi networks when approving sensitive government transactions. If you must use public Wi-Fi, consider using a trusted virtual private network to encrypt your connection.

Regularly review the list of active sessions and linked services in the Nafath app. If you see an unfamiliar device or service, remove it immediately and report the issue. Being proactive about security reduces the risk of identity theft and fraud.

Nafath and the Future of Government Services

Nafath is expected to become the default login method for an increasing number of Saudi government and semi-government services. As Vision 2030 progresses, more transactions will move from physical offices to digital platforms. Nafath provides the trust layer that makes this shift possible.

For residents, this means faster service, fewer trips to government offices, and a more consistent user experience. New services in healthcare, education, housing, and business licensing may adopt Nafath authentication. Staying familiar with the platform prepares you for these changes.

Because the platform is still evolving, users should follow official announcements from the National Information Center and the Digital Government Authority. These sources publish updates about new features, supported services, and any changes to registration requirements. Relying on official channels protects you from misinformation and scams.

Nafath for Business Accounts and Delegation

Companies and institutions in Saudi Arabia can use Nafath for authorized representatives. A business account may be linked to the national ID or iqama of an employee who has legal authority to act on behalf of the organization. This is useful for government portals related to taxes, labor, commerce, and municipal services.

Delegation must be set up carefully. The organization’s administrator assigns roles and permissions, and the delegated user authenticates through Nafath. This creates an audit trail showing who accessed each service and when. Businesses should review delegated access regularly and remove former employees immediately.

If you manage government transactions for a company, make sure your personal Nafath account is active and secure. Coordinate with your organization’s IT or legal team before linking accounts. Proper delegation prevents unauthorized actions and protects both the employee and the company.

Accessibility and Special-Needs Users

Nafath supports accessibility features that help users with disabilities. The mobile app works with screen readers on modern smartphones. Biometric options such as fingerprint and Face ID reduce the need to type complex passwords, which benefits users with motor impairments.

If you or a family member has difficulty using the standard app, contact Nafath support or visit a service center. Staff can explain alternative authentication methods and help set up the account. The Communications, Space and Technology Commission also provides channels to report accessibility problems.

Older users who are not comfortable with smartphones may need help from a trusted family member during registration. It is important that the account remains under the user’s own identity, even if someone else assists with setup. Never register an account in another person’s name without authorization.

Fixing Registration and Verification Errors

Sometimes Nafath cannot verify a user automatically. Common causes include a mismatch between the ID number and phone records, an expired iqama, or missing biometric data. Start by checking that your mobile number is registered under your name with your telecom provider. A number registered under a sponsor or company name may cause problems.

Next, verify that your Absher profile is active and up to date. Nafath relies on Absher for some identity checks. If Absher shows outdated information, correct it before retrying Nafath. If you recently renewed your iqama, wait a day or two for systems to sync.

If automatic verification continues to fail, visit a Nafath activation point or Jawazat office. Bring your iqama, passport, and the phone you want to register. Staff can complete the setup manually and explain any issues.

Nafath vs SMS One-Time Passwords

Some services still offer login by SMS one-time password. While convenient, SMS codes can be intercepted or forwarded by attackers. Nafath is generally more secure because it sends the login request directly to your registered device and requires biometric or PIN approval.

However, Nafath depends on your phone having internet access and notifications enabled. If you are traveling or in an area with poor data coverage, SMS may still work. In practice, many government services now prefer or require Nafath for high-risk transactions.

Use SMS only when Nafath is unavailable, and never share an SMS code with anyone. Treat SMS codes as sensitive information. If you receive an unexpected SMS code, someone may be trying to access your account. Change your password and review your account activity.

What Is Next for Nafath

The Saudi government plans to expand Nafath to cover more services and add features such as digital identity credentials. In the future, Nafath may be used to sign documents electronically, access health records, and verify age for regulated services. These developments will make the platform even more central to daily life.

Users should keep their app updated to benefit from new features and security improvements. Official announcements will explain how to activate new services. Be cautious of unofficial apps or websites claiming to offer enhanced Nafath features.

As digital identity becomes more important, protecting your Nafath account is as important as protecting your passport. Use strong authentication, monitor account activity, and report suspicious behavior promptly.

Nafath for Senior Users and Less Tech-Savvy People

Older residents and those who are less comfortable with technology may find Nafath confusing at first. The app uses small text and multiple steps that can be difficult for some users. Family members can help by installing the app, registering the account, and practicing the login process together.

Using a phone with a large screen and enabling accessibility features such as magnification and voice guidance can make the app easier to use. Set up fingerprint login if the phone supports it, so the user does not need to remember a long password.

Write down the steps for common tasks in simple language. Keep a printed note with the phone number for Nafath support. Patience and practice help users become confident with the platform over time.

Reporting Lost or Stolen Devices

If your phone with Nafath is lost or stolen, act quickly. Use another device to log into your accounts and change passwords. Contact your bank if you use mobile banking. Notify your mobile operator to suspend the SIM and prevent calls or messages.

Contact Nafath support or visit a service center to report the lost device. They can help deactivate the app on that device. Even if someone has your phone, they cannot approve login requests without your biometric or PIN, but deactivating the device adds extra protection.

When you get a replacement phone, reinstall the Nafath app and register the new device. You may need to verify your identity again. Update your registered phone number with your bank and government services to match the new SIM.

Using Nafath While Traveling Abroad

If you travel outside Saudi Arabia, you can still use Nafath as long as your phone has internet access. Login requests will arrive as push notifications wherever you are. Make sure roaming data is active or connect to a trusted Wi-Fi network before approving sensitive transactions.

Some government services may restrict access from foreign IP addresses for security reasons. If you cannot log in, try using a Saudi VPN or wait until you return. Avoid using public hotel computers for government services, as they may not be secure.

Before long trips, inform your bank and update any travel notifications. This reduces the chance that financial transactions are blocked while you are abroad. Keeping Nafath active ensures you can still manage important accounts.

Managing Nafath Notifications

Nafath relies on notifications to alert you to login requests. If notifications are disabled, delayed, or silenced, you may miss important requests. In the phone settings, allow notifications for the Nafath app and enable sounds or banners so you notice them.

If you receive too many notifications because of frequent logins, review which services are linked. Logging out of unused services reduces notification volume. You can also check the app’s history to see recent login activity.

Never approve a notification you did not expect. If a request appears while you are not trying to log in, reject it immediately. This is a sign that someone may have your password or is attempting unauthorized access.

Nafath Help and Official Resources

For official help with Nafath, use the support channels listed on the Nafath website or inside the app. The help center answers common questions about registration, login, and troubleshooting. You can also contact support by phone or chat during business hours.

Service centers and Absher kiosks can assist with identity verification problems. Bring your ID, iqama, and registered phone. Staff can complete setup manually and reset account settings if needed.

Avoid unofficial forums and social media advice for security-related issues. Scammers may pose as support agents. Always verify that you are communicating through official channels before sharing personal information.

Frequently asked questions

What is Nafath used for?

Nafath is a unified national login system that lets users access many Saudi government services with one digital identity.

Is Nafath the same as Absher?

No. Absher is a service portal, while Nafath is an identity and authentication layer used by multiple platforms.

How do I register for Nafath?

Download the Nafath app, enter your ID or iqama number, verify your phone, and set up biometric or PIN authentication.

What if I do not receive the login notification?

Check your internet and notifications, then use the one-time code option inside the Nafath app.

Can I use Nafath on multiple devices?

You can register a new device, but you may need to verify your identity again. Remove old devices from your account.

Is Nafath secure?

Yes, when used correctly. Enable biometrics, keep the app updated, and never approve unexpected requests.

Where can I find the official Nafath app?

Download it from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store. Use only the official app published by the National Information Center.
