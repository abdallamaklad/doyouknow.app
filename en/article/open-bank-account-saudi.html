How to Open a Bank Account in Saudi Arabia

doyouknow.app Editorial Team2026-07-094 min read

Editorial note: This guide was checked against Saudi Central Bank rules for individual bank accounts and official Saudi bank information on 2026-07-09. Requirements can differ by bank, so verify the current conditions before visiting a branch.

If you have just arrived in Saudi Arabia, a bank account is one of the first practical steps you need. Without it, salaries, rent payments, online shopping, and daily transfers become difficult. Saudi banks offer strong digital apps and modern services, but you still need the correct identity documents and account type.

Why you need a Saudi bank account

Most employees receive salaries through a Saudi bank account. Residents also use bank accounts for rent, bills, government payments, cards, and transfers. The Saudi banking sector is supervised by the Saudi Central Bank, known as SAMA, which sets customer-protection and compliance rules.

Popular banks for residents

Saudi National Bank

Saudi National Bank is one of the largest banks in the Kingdom. It is widely used for salary accounts, personal accounts, and cards, and it is often convenient for employees whose companies have established banking relationships.

Al Rajhi Bank

Al Rajhi is a major Islamic bank with a highly used mobile app. It is a common choice for customers who prefer Islamic banking products and a broad branch and ATM network.

Bank Albilad

Bank Albilad is another Islamic bank with modern digital services, investment account options, and personal and small-business products.

Other banks

The Arabic source also highlights Riyad Bank and SAB as common options for residents who want broad commercial banking, customer service, credit cards, and personal finance products.

Types of bank accounts

  • Salary account: usually opened through an employer so salary can be transferred automatically.
  • Current account: used for deposits, withdrawals, bill payments, international transfers, and everyday banking.
  • Savings account: used for saving, sometimes with profit or investment return depending on the bank and product.
  • Visitor or foreigner account: some banks may provide limited accounts for people without an iqama, but services are restricted until residency is issued.

Documents usually required

  • A valid iqama for residents.
  • A valid passport copy, with the original sometimes requested for verification.
  • A salary certificate or employer/sponsor letter if you are employed.
  • A Saudi mobile number registered in your name.
  • Recent personal photos if requested by the bank.
  • A national address registered in the Saudi address system.

Opening an account before receiving an iqama

If you are a visitor or a new worker who has not received an iqama yet, some banks may allow a temporary or limited account. The Arabic source lists the usual documents as a valid passport, valid visa, proof letter from the employer or sponsor, and a Saudi mobile number. The account remains limited until the iqama is issued and the bank upgrades it to a full account.

Step-by-step process

  1. Choose the bank: compare account fees, app quality, ATM network, branch access, salary-transfer convenience, and international-transfer options.
  2. Prepare the documents: bring the iqama, passport, salary or sponsor letter, national address, registered Saudi mobile number, and any photos requested.
  3. Register through the app or visit a branch: many banks allow starting online, but some cases still require branch verification.
  4. Verify identity: the bank may verify through Nafath, SMS codes, branch documents, or employee review.
  5. Receive card and activate digital banking: activate the Mada card, online banking, app login, and transfer limits.

Fees and practical notes

Basic personal accounts are often free, but charges may apply for international transfers, replacement cards, special statements, premium cards, or certain financing products. Review the bank's fee schedule before opening the account. Keep your mobile number active because Saudi banking relies heavily on SMS and app verification.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Visiting a branch without a registered Saudi mobile number.
  • Using an address that does not match the national address record.
  • Assuming a visitor account has the same features as a resident account.
  • Ignoring international-transfer fees if you send money home frequently.
  • Choosing a bank only because it is famous instead of matching your salary, branch, and app needs.

Frequently asked questions

Can a foreigner open a bank account in Saudi Arabia?

Yes. A resident normally needs a valid iqama, passport, registered Saudi mobile number, and employer or sponsor documentation where required.

Can I open an account without an iqama?

Some banks may allow a temporary limited account with a passport, visa, sponsor or employer letter, and Saudi mobile number. Full services usually require an iqama.

Which bank is best?

There is no single best bank. Compare your salary arrangement, branch access, mobile app, fees, and international-transfer needs.
